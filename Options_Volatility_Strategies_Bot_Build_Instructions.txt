SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

This strategy harvests the persistent gap between implied volatility (IV) and
realized volatility (RV) in cryptocurrency options markets. Crypto IV
consistently overprices actual volatility (negative variance risk premium),
creating a structural edge for volatility sellers. The strategy primarily
sells options (covered calls, cash-secured puts, strangles) while maintaining
delta-neutral exposure through dynamic hedging.

NOTE: Binance does not offer a native options market with the same depth as
Deribit. This strategy is designed to operate on Binance via SYNTHETIC options
positions constructed from perpetual futures and spot, OR to interface with
Deribit as a secondary exchange if available. The instructions below specify
the logic for both approaches. If only Binance is available, the strategy
operates in Synthetic Mode (covered calls and volatility estimation only).

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- High implied volatility environments (IV > 60% annualized for BTC)
- Periods where IV significantly exceeds recent realized volatility
  (IV/RV ratio > 1.3)
- Post-event IV crush: after anticipated events (FOMC, CPI, halvings), IV
  drops sharply — selling options before the event captures this decay
- Range-bound markets where theta decay works in the seller's favor

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- Crypto's negative variance risk premium is academically documented
- IV consistently exceeds RV by 10-30% on average
- BTC front-end ATM implied volatility traded between 40% and 95%+ in 2025
- Professional funds using volatility strategies achieve Sharpe ratios > 2.0
- The edge is SELLING overpriced insurance (options premium) to the market

1.4 SUB-STRATEGIES

Sub-Strategy A: Covered Call Writing
- Hold long spot BTC/ETH, sell OTM call options against the position
- Generates premium income with capped upside
- Synthetic implementation on Binance: hold spot + sell futures at higher
  price (simulates call selling through price-conditional futures orders)

Sub-Strategy B: Cash-Secured Put Selling
- Sell OTM put options, holding USDT as collateral
- Collects premium; if exercised, buys the asset at a discount
- Synthetic on Binance: set limit buy orders at target levels with premium
  calculation embedded in the position sizing

Sub-Strategy C: Delta-Neutral Volatility Selling (Deribit Required)
- Sell ATM straddles or OTM strangles on Deribit
- Delta-hedge using Binance perpetual futures
- Profit from theta decay when realized vol < implied vol

Sub-Strategy D: Volatility Arbitrage (Deribit Required)
- Compare IV across strikes and expirations for mispricing
- Sell overpriced IV, buy underpriced IV (vol surface arbitrage)
- Delta-hedge the net position

1.5 TARGET INSTRUMENTS

- BTC and ETH ONLY (highest options liquidity)
- Options: BTC-USD and ETH-USD options on Deribit (if available)
- Hedging: BTCUSDT and ETHUSDT perpetual futures on Binance
- Spot: BTC and ETH on Binance (for covered call collateral)

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 VOLATILITY REGIME ASSESSMENT (RUN EVERY HOUR)

Step 1: Calculate Realized Volatility
- Use 1-minute close-to-close returns over rolling windows:
  RV_1d = stdev(1-min returns) × sqrt(1440) × 100  (annualized, 1-day window)
  RV_7d = stdev(1-min returns) × sqrt(1440) × 100  (7-day window)
  RV_30d = stdev(1-min returns) × sqrt(1440) × 100 (30-day window)

Step 2: Estimate Implied Volatility
If Deribit available: use ATM IV directly from options chain
If Deribit NOT available (Synthetic Mode): estimate IV from the Binance
futures basis and funding rate:
  IV_estimate = sqrt(2π) × |Futures_Premium| / sqrt(T) × adjustment_factor
  Where T = time to next funding in years, adjustment_factor calibrated
  historically to match Deribit IV (approximately 1.5-2.0)
  This is a ROUGH estimate — flag in all logs as "estimated IV"

Step 3: IV/RV Ratio
- IV_RV_Ratio = IV / RV_7d
- If IV_RV_Ratio > 1.3: Volatility is overpriced — FAVORABLE for selling
- If IV_RV_Ratio > 1.5: Strongly overpriced — increase position sizes
- If IV_RV_Ratio < 1.1: Volatility is fairly priced — REDUCE activity
- If IV_RV_Ratio < 1.0: Volatility is underpriced — DO NOT sell vol

3.2 SUB-STRATEGY A: COVERED CALL ENTRY

Conditions:
- Hold a spot BTC/ETH position (or open one at market)
- IV_RV_Ratio > 1.2
- Daily trend is neutral to slightly bullish (EMA 20 > EMA 50 on daily,
  but price not more than 1 daily ATR above 20 EMA — avoiding strong trends
  where upside capture is more valuable than premium)

Execution (Synthetic on Binance):
- Calculate the "strike price" equivalent: Current_Price × (1 + target_OTM%)
  Default target_OTM: 5% above current price
  Adjustment: if IV is high (> 80%), use 8% OTM (collect more premium from
  a wider strike); if IV is moderate (50-80%), use 5% OTM
- Calculate "premium" equivalent: using Black-Scholes approximation with
  estimated IV, time to expiration (default: 7 days), and the strike
  Premium ≈ Price × IV × sqrt(T) × N'(d1) (simplified ATM approximation,
  adjust for OTM using full BS formula)
- Place a LIMIT SELL order on futures at the strike price with:
  Quantity = spot holding quantity
  Type: TAKE_PROFIT_MARKET (triggers when price reaches strike)
  This simulates the call being exercised — spot is effectively "called away"
- The "premium" is tracked as theoretical income from the strategy

Expiration simulation:
- If price does not reach the strike within the "expiration" window (7 days):
  Cancel the sell order — premium is "kept" (add to theoretical PnL)
  Set up a new covered call for the next period
- If price reaches the strike: the sell order fills, spot is sold
  Net result: profit = (Strike − Entry) + Premium (theoretical)
  Re-purchase spot if still bullish, or wait for a pullback

3.3 SUB-STRATEGY B: CASH-SECURED PUT ENTRY

Conditions:
- Holding USDT (not deployed in other strategies)
- IV_RV_Ratio > 1.2
- Asset is in an uptrend or neutral (not in a crash — selling puts into a
  crash is extremely risky)
- RSI(14) daily > 40 (not oversold — put selling into weakness is dangerous)

Execution (Synthetic on Binance):
- Calculate strike: Current_Price × (1 − target_OTM%)
  Default target_OTM: 5% below current price
- Calculate premium: Black-Scholes put premium estimate
- Place a LIMIT BUY order at the strike price:
  Quantity = Cash_Available / Strike_Price
  This simulates put exercise — if price drops to strike, asset is purchased
- Reserve USDT = Strike × Quantity (this is the "cash-secured" part)

Expiration simulation:
- If price does not drop to strike within window: cancel buy order, keep
  theoretical premium
- If price drops and buy fills: position acquired at effective discount
  (Strike − Premium). Manage the acquired position according to STRAT-001
  or STRAT-004 rules.

3.4 SUB-STRATEGY C: DELTA-NEUTRAL STRADDLE/STRANGLE (DERIBIT REQUIRED)

Conditions:
- IV_RV_Ratio > 1.4 (strong premium to capture)
- No major events within the option expiration window
- Sufficient Deribit account balance

Execution:
- Sell ATM straddle (sell 1 ATM call + sell 1 ATM put) on Deribit
  OR sell OTM strangle (sell OTM call + sell OTM put) for wider breakevens
- Calculate initial delta of the combined position
- Hedge delta on Binance perpetual futures:
  If delta is positive (long bias): short futures
  If delta is negative (short bias): long futures
  Hedge ratio: position_delta × notional = futures hedge quantity

Delta Rebalancing:
- Monitor delta continuously via Deribit Greeks stream
- Rebalance when delta exceeds ±0.10 per lot sold
- Rebalance by adjusting Binance futures position
- Maximum rebalances per day: 20 (fee-constrained)
- Each rebalance should bring delta within ±0.03

3.5 ENTRY TIMING

For all sub-strategies:
- Prefer entering when IV is spiking (before events, during fear)
- The 30 minutes BEFORE a known event is often the IV peak — ideal entry
  for selling (IV will crush after the event regardless of direction)
- Do NOT enter during the first 30 minutes after market-moving news (let
  IV and prices stabilize)

SECTION 4: EXIT LOGIC
================================================================================

4.1 COVERED CALL EXIT
- Strike reached (call "exercised"): spot sold, profit + premium captured
- Expiration (7 days): cancel sell order, keep premium, set up new cycle
- Early exit: if underlying drops > 1 daily ATR, consider closing the
  spot position to protect downside (the short call was providing partial
  hedge via premium, but may not be enough in a crash)
- If IV drops below RV_7d while holding covered call: close early (IV crush
  captured, remaining time value is minimal)

4.2 CASH-SECURED PUT EXIT
- Strike reached (put "exercised"): buy fills, manage as new position
- Expiration: cancel buy order, keep premium, set up new cycle
- Early exit: if underlying crashes > 2 daily ATR and approaching strike,
  consider cancelling the buy order to avoid being forced into a rapidly
  falling asset. Accept the missed "premium" to avoid a larger loss.

4.3 DELTA-NEUTRAL EXIT (DERIBIT)
- Time-based: Close 50% of the position when 50% of time to expiration has
  passed (capture most theta decay, reduce gamma risk near expiry)
- Profit target: Close when 60% of maximum theoretical profit (total premium
  collected) is realized
- Stop loss: Close if unrealized loss exceeds 100% of premium collected
  (the sold options have doubled in value — vol has moved against you)
- Delta hedge unwind: when options are closed, immediately close the Binance
  futures hedge

4.4 VOLATILITY EXIT TRIGGER
- If RV exceeds the IV at which options were sold (realized vol > implied vol
  at entry), the trade is LOSING its edge. Close within 4 hours.
- Monitor this continuously: RV_rolling > IV_at_entry → exit signal

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 POSITION SIZING & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital for this strategy: 25% of total equity
- Sub-strategy allocation:
  Covered calls: up to 15% (limited by spot holdings)
  Cash-secured puts: up to 10%
  Delta-neutral: up to 15% (Deribit collateral + Binance hedge margin)
  Total may exceed 25% due to overlap (covered call uses spot that could
  be collateral for puts) — the 25% limit applies to MAX RISK CAPITAL

5.2 MAXIMUM LOSS PER TRADE
- Covered call: max loss = spot depreciation − premium (effectively unlimited
  downside on spot). Hard stop on spot: 10% below entry.
- Cash-secured put: max loss = strike price × quantity − premium (the full
  purchase value if asset goes to zero). Hard stop: cancel buy order if
  underlying drops > 15% from current price.
- Delta-neutral: max loss = 2× premium collected. Close at this level.

5.3 GREEK LIMITS (DERIBIT MODE)

- Maximum net delta: ±0.15 per $10,000 notional (hedge when exceeded)
- Maximum gamma: −0.005 per dollar of premium sold (avoid excessive near-term
  expiration risk)
- Maximum vega: −$500 per percentage point of IV (limit sensitivity to vol)
- Maximum theta: positive and uncapped (theta is income)
- Monitor Greeks continuously; breach of ANY limit triggers rebalance or exit

5.4 DRAWDOWN PROTECTION

Daily: 2% halt
Weekly: 4%
Monthly: 7%
System-wide: 15%

5.5 EVENT RISK MANAGEMENT

- If a major event is within 24 hours AND the strategy has open vol-selling
  positions: tighten delta hedges to ±0.05 (not ±0.10)
- If event is within 2 hours: close 50% of vol-selling positions (event
  gamma risk is extreme for vol sellers near expiry)
- Do NOT sell new volatility within 2 hours before major scheduled events

5.6 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

IV Spike: If IV jumps > 20 percentage points in 4 hours (e.g., from 60% to
80%), close all vol-selling positions immediately. This suggests a volatility
regime change that invalidates the short-vol thesis.

Flash Crash: If underlying drops > 8% in 1 hour, close all positions.
Delta hedges may not keep up with gamma during crashes.

Kill Switch: Close all Binance positions (spot, futures) and Deribit options.

SECTION 6: POSITION SIZING RULES
================================================================================

6.1 COVERED CALL SIZING
- Sell calls against 100% of spot holding (1:1 ratio)
- Do NOT sell more calls than spot held (naked calls = unlimited risk)

6.2 CASH-SECURED PUT SIZING
- Put obligation = Strike × Quantity
- Must have ≥ 100% of obligation in USDT (fully cash-secured)
- Never sell puts on margin (unlimited downside)

6.3 DELTA-NEUTRAL SIZING
- Premium collected should not exceed 3% of strategy equity per trade
- Maximum total premium at risk: 8% of strategy equity across all open trades
- Delta hedge size determined by option Greeks, not by capital rules

6.4 LEVERAGE
- Covered calls: no leverage (spot-backed)
- Cash-secured puts: no leverage (USDT-backed)
- Delta hedging on Binance: max 3x leverage on futures leg
- Delta-neutral Deribit: use Deribit's margin rules (typically 10-20x for
  option sellers with hedges)

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 IV/RV RATIO FILTER
- Must exceed 1.2 before any vol-selling activity
- If below 1.0, no vol selling under any circumstances

7.2 TREND FILTER FOR COVERED CALLS
- Only write covered calls if trend is neutral or mildly bullish
- In strong uptrends (ADX > 30 with bullish direction), do NOT write calls
  (opportunity cost of capped upside is too high)

7.3 CRASH FILTER FOR PUT SELLING
- Do not sell puts if underlying has dropped > 10% in 7 days
- Do not sell puts if RSI(14) daily < 30 (oversold crash conditions)

7.4 EXPIRATION FILTER
- Do NOT sell options with less than 3 days to expiration (gamma risk too high)
- Do NOT sell options with more than 30 days to expiration (theta decay too
  slow, capital tied up)
- Sweet spot: 7-14 days to expiration

7.5 LIQUIDITY FILTER (DERIBIT)
- Bid-ask spread on target options must be < 5% of option mid-price
- Open interest > 100 contracts for the target strike

7.6 CONSECUTIVE LOSS HANDLING
- After 3 consecutive losing vol-selling cycles: halt for 14 days
  (Extended halt because vol regime changes can persist for weeks)
- Review IV/RV calculation methodology before resuming

SECTION 9: PAPER TRADING SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

9.1 OPTIONS PRICING SIMULATION

- Use Black-Scholes model for European option pricing:
  d1 = [ln(S/K) + (r + σ²/2)T] / (σ√T)
  d2 = d1 − σ√T
  Call = S×N(d1) − K×e^(−rT)×N(d2)
  Put = K×e^(−rT)×N(−d2) − S×N(−d1)
- Use actual RV for σ in simulation (more conservative than using IV)
- Risk-free rate r = 5% annualized (configurable)

9.2 DELTA HEDGE SIMULATION

- Simulate delta hedging costs: every rebalance incurs taker fees on Binance
- Track cumulative hedge PnL separately from option premium income

9.3 GO-LIVE CRITERIA (90-day minimum — longer due to strategy complexity)

- Net positive PnL after all simulated fees and hedge costs
- Profit factor > 1.3
- Maximum drawdown < 5%
- IV/RV regime detection correctly avoided < 1.0 ratio periods
- Delta stayed within limits > 90% of the time
- Covered call premium income exceeded hedge costs by > 2x

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 SUDDEN IV EXPANSION (VOL SQUEEZE)
- If IV spikes 30%+ while holding short vol positions, unrealized losses
  mount rapidly. This is the primary risk.
- Circuit breaker at 20% IV jump handles this (Section 5.6)
- Post-event: wait for IV to stabilize (typically 24-48 hours) before re-entering

11.2 GAP THROUGH COVERED CALL STRIKE
- Price gaps up through the call strike: spot is sold, upside missed
- This is an expected outcome, not a failure — premium was collected
- Re-establish spot position if desired, set up next cycle

11.3 ASSIGNMENT ON PUT (DERIBIT) DURING CRASH
- If price crashes below put strike: the put is exercised, asset is purchased
  at the strike price minus premium
- If the crash continues, the acquired asset loses further value
- Hard stop at 15% below strike price limits the damage

11.4 DELTA HEDGE LAG
- During very fast moves, delta hedging on Binance may lag option delta changes
- This creates temporary unhedged exposure
- Mitigated by: frequent rebalancing (when vol is high, rebalance more often),
  using wider delta tolerance bands, and avoiding gamma-heavy positions
  (near-expiry ATM options)

11.5 DERIBIT CONNECTIVITY LOSS
- If Deribit connection is lost while Binance hedge is active:
  The Binance hedge position is now unmanaged (delta may drift)
  Close Binance hedge to zero if Deribit is unreachable for > 5 minutes
  This converts the position from "hedged vol sale" to "unhedged vol sale"
  (much riskier) — accept the risk or close Deribit positions ASAP
