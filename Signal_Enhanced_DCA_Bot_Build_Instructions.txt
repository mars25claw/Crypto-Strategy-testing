SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

This strategy enhances traditional dollar-cost averaging by dynamically
adjusting purchase amounts based on quantitative signals. Instead of buying
a fixed dollar amount at fixed intervals (vanilla DCA), the bot buys MORE
when conditions indicate favorable prices (oversold, extreme fear, below key
moving averages) and LESS when conditions indicate overvaluation (overbought,
extreme greed, extended above key moving averages). It is a long-term
accumulation strategy, not a trading strategy.

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- ALL market conditions (DCA is designed to work through bull and bear cycles)
- The signal enhancement specifically targets: buying heavier during crashes,
  corrections, and fear; buying lighter during euphoria and overextension
- Best suited for long-term accumulation (6+ month horizon minimum)

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- 5-year weekly DCA into BTC ($10/week from 2019-2024) produced 202% return
- Signal-enhanced DCA using Fear & Greed contrarian approach produced 1,145%
  cumulative return (2018-2025), outperforming buy-and-hold by 99 percentage
  points
- Value averaging (adjusting amounts to target fixed growth) achieves lower
  average cost than standard DCA
- The mathematical edge: buying more at lower prices and less at higher prices
  GUARANTEES a lower average cost than fixed-amount DCA (assuming price
  eventually reverts or appreciates)

1.4 TARGET INSTRUMENTS

Primary: BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT (SPOT only — no futures for DCA)
Secondary: SOL/USDT, BNB/USDT (optional, configurable)
Maximum instruments: 4
All purchases are on SPOT market, held in spot wallet

1.5 TIMEFRAMES

DCA interval: Configurable (daily, weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)
Default: WEEKLY (every Monday at 10:00 UTC)
Signal calculation: Daily (update signals once per day at 00:00 UTC)
Monitoring: Hourly (for crash-buy triggers and health checks)

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 SCHEDULED DCA — BASE PURCHASE

At every DCA interval (default: Monday 10:00 UTC):

Step 1: Calculate Base Amount
- Base_Amount_USDT = configured recurring investment amount
  Default: configurable, e.g., $100/week per instrument
  This is the STANDARD amount when all signals are neutral

Step 2: Calculate Signal Multiplier
The signal multiplier adjusts the base amount up or down based on market
conditions. It ranges from 0.25x (buy very little) to 3.0x (buy aggressively).

Signal A: RSI(14) on Daily Candles
- RSI < 25:  Multiplier component = 1.5 (deeply oversold, buy more)
- RSI 25-35: Multiplier component = 1.25
- RSI 35-50: Multiplier component = 1.0 (neutral)
- RSI 50-65: Multiplier component = 0.85
- RSI 65-75: Multiplier component = 0.7 (overbought, buy less)
- RSI > 75:  Multiplier component = 0.5 (extremely overbought, buy minimum)

Signal B: Fear & Greed Index
- Value 0-10 (Extreme Fear):   Multiplier component = 1.75
- Value 11-25 (Fear):          Multiplier component = 1.4
- Value 26-45 (Moderate Fear):  Multiplier component = 1.1
- Value 46-55 (Neutral):       Multiplier component = 1.0
- Value 56-75 (Greed):         Multiplier component = 0.75
- Value 76-90 (High Greed):    Multiplier component = 0.5
- Value 91-100 (Extreme Greed): Multiplier component = 0.25

Signal C: Price vs 200-Day SMA
- Price < 200 SMA × 0.80 (>20% below SMA): Multiplier component = 1.5
- Price < 200 SMA × 0.90 (10-20% below):    Multiplier component = 1.25
- Price < 200 SMA (below SMA):              Multiplier component = 1.1
- Price > 200 SMA (above SMA):              Multiplier component = 0.9
- Price > 200 SMA × 1.20 (>20% above):       Multiplier component = 0.75
- Price > 200 SMA × 1.40 (>40% above):       Multiplier component = 0.5

Signal D: SOPR (if available)
- SOPR < 0.95: Multiplier component = 1.3 (market selling at heavy loss)
- SOPR 0.95-1.0: Multiplier component = 1.1 (selling at slight loss)
- SOPR 1.0-1.05: Multiplier component = 1.0 (neutral)
- SOPR > 1.05: Multiplier component = 0.85 (market selling at profit)

Step 3: Combine Multipliers
Final_Multiplier = geometric mean of all available signal components
= (Component_A × Component_B × Component_C × Component_D)^(1/N)
where N = number of active signals (3 if no on-chain, 4 if available)

Clamp Final_Multiplier between 0.25 and 3.0

Step 4: Calculate Purchase Amount
Purchase_Amount = Base_Amount × Final_Multiplier

Step 5: Budget Verification
- Monthly budget cap: 4.5 × Base_Amount × Num_Intervals_Per_Month
  (allows up to 4.5x average spending, not infinite)
- If Purchase_Amount would exceed remaining monthly budget: cap at remaining
- If remaining monthly budget is less than minimum purchase: skip this interval

Step 6: Execute Purchase
- Order type: MARKET buy on spot
- Timing: execute at the scheduled time (no timing optimization for DCA)
- Log: purchase amount USDT, quantity received, execution price, signal
  multiplier, individual signal values

3.2 CRASH-BUY TRIGGER (OPPORTUNISTIC)

In addition to scheduled DCA, deploy extra capital during sharp crashes:

Detection:
- If price drops > 10% from its 7-day high: Level 1 crash-buy trigger
- If price drops > 20% from its 30-day high: Level 2 crash-buy trigger
- If price drops > 30% from its 90-day high: Level 3 crash-buy trigger
- Check hourly using 1h klines

Crash-Buy Amounts:
- Level 1: 1.0 × weekly Base_Amount as bonus purchase
- Level 2: 2.0 × weekly Base_Amount as bonus purchase
- Level 3: 3.0 × weekly Base_Amount as bonus purchase

Cooldown:
- Level 1: can trigger once per 7 days
- Level 2: can trigger once per 14 days
- Level 3: can trigger once per 30 days
- If multiple levels trigger simultaneously: execute ONLY the highest level

Monthly Budget Impact:
- Crash-buys come from a SEPARATE budget: 2× monthly DCA budget reserved
  for crash-buy opportunities
- If crash-buy budget is exhausted: no more crash-buys until next month

Execution:
- Place MARKET buy order immediately on detection
- Do NOT wait for further decline (do not try to time the bottom)
- Log with crash-buy identifier and level

3.3 VALUE AVERAGING MODE (ALTERNATIVE TO SIGNAL-ENHANCED DCA)

This is an ALTERNATIVE mode (configurable, not active simultaneously with
signal-enhanced DCA):

- Set a target portfolio growth rate: e.g., $100/week increase in total value
- At each interval:
  Target_Value = Initial_Value + (Week_Number × Weekly_Target)
  Current_Value = Holdings × Current_Price
  Required_Purchase = Target_Value − Current_Value
  If Required_Purchase > 0: buy that amount
  If Required_Purchase < 0: do NOT sell (DCA never sells, only buys less)
  Minimum purchase: $0 (skip if portfolio is already above target)
  Maximum purchase: 3 × Weekly_Target (cap to prevent excessive buying in crashes)

================================================================================

4.1 THIS STRATEGY DOES NOT ACTIVELY EXIT

DCA is a long-term accumulation strategy. It does NOT have standard exit logic.

4.2 MANUAL EXIT (OPERATOR-TRIGGERED ONLY)

- The operator decides to take profits or liquidate
- Execution: MARKET sell or staged LIMIT sells over 24-48 hours
- This is NEVER automated — always requires manual dashboard action

4.3 EMERGENCY STOP-LOSS (OPTIONAL, CONFIGURABLE)

If the operator configures a portfolio-level stop loss:
- Trigger: total DCA portfolio value drops below X% of total invested amount
  Default: 50% (i.e., portfolio is worth less than half of amount invested)
- Action: HALT new purchases. Do NOT auto-sell (selling at 50% loss locks in
  the loss; DCA philosophy is to continue buying through corrections)
- Alert operator with current portfolio value and signal assessment
- This is a PAUSE mechanism, not an auto-liquidation

4.4 TAKE-PROFIT REBALANCING (OPTIONAL, CONFIGURABLE)

If configured, when total portfolio value exceeds invested amount by X%:
- Default threshold: 200% (portfolio 3× invested amount)
- Action: sell 10% of holdings to realize partial profits
- Transfer realized profits to USDT reserve (available for crash-buy or
  other strategies)
- This is a GENTLE rebalancing, not a full exit
- Maximum rebalancing frequency: once per quarter

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital for this strategy: 30% of total account equity
- This capital is deployed GRADUALLY over time (not all at once)
- Monthly DCA budget: configurable, default $400/month for primary instruments
  + $200/month crash-buy reserve = $600/month total
- The strategy's capital footprint grows over time as assets are accumulated

5.2 DIVERSIFICATION

- No single asset should exceed 60% of DCA portfolio value
  If BTC appreciation causes it to exceed 60%, reduce BTC DCA allocation
  and increase other instruments until rebalanced
- Minimum 2 instruments (BTC + ETH at minimum)

5.3 DRAWDOWN MANAGEMENT

- This strategy does NOT use traditional drawdown stops because DCA
  EXPECTS to buy through drawdowns
- Instead, use a "conviction monitor":
  If portfolio is down > 30% from peak invested value AND all signals
  indicate extreme fear: INCREASE purchase amounts (this is the strategy
  working as designed)
  If portfolio is down > 50%: halt and alert operator (as per Section 4.3)

5.4 BUDGET DISCIPLINE

- Monthly budget is a HARD LIMIT (including crash-buys)
- Total_Monthly_Spend ≤ Monthly_DCA_Budget + Monthly_Crash_Reserve
- The bot must track spending per calendar month and refuse purchases
  that exceed the budget
- Budget resets at 00:00 UTC on the 1st of each month

5.5 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

Exchange Issue:
- If spot order fails 3 times: halt DCA for 24 hours, alert operator
- If balance is insufficient for minimum purchase: alert operator (funding
  needed)

Data Feed Issue:
- If Fear & Greed API is unavailable: use internal RSI-only multiplier
  (reduce to 2 signal components)
- If kline data is stale (> 2 hours old): delay purchase until fresh data

Kill Switch:
- Cancels any pending orders (rare for DCA — most are market orders)
- Does NOT liquidate holdings (DCA holdings are long-term)
- Halts all future scheduled purchases until manual restart

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 SCHEDULE ADHERENCE
- Do NOT deviate from the scheduled interval by more than 1 hour
  (If the bot is down at scheduled time, execute within 1 hour of restart)
- If more than 1 hour late: skip this interval, execute at next scheduled time

7.2 BUDGET FILTER
- Before every purchase: verify remaining monthly budget ≥ purchase amount
- If not: reduce to remaining budget or skip if below minimum

7.3 EXCHANGE HEALTH FILTER
- Before every purchase: verify spot order book has reasonable spread (< 0.1%)
- If spread is abnormally wide: delay purchase by 1 hour and re-check

7.4 SIGNAL DATA FRESHNESS
- RSI must be calculated from data no older than 4 hours
- Fear & Greed index must be from today (API updates daily)
- 200 SMA must be calculated from data no older than 24 hours
- If any signal data is stale: use last known valid value with a log warning

7.5 NO CONSECUTIVE LOSS HANDLING
- DCA does not have "losses" in the traditional sense — purchases are not
  individual trades with P&L
- Instead, track the rolling cost basis vs current value
- If cost basis is > 40% above current value: alert operator, continue DCA
  unless emergency stop is triggered

SECTION 10: PERFORMANCE METRICS
================================================================================

10.1 STANDARD DCA METRICS (NOT standard trading metrics)

- Total Invested: cumulative USDT spent on purchases
- Current Portfolio Value: holdings × current price per instrument
- Total Return: (Current_Value − Total_Invested) / Total_Invested × 100%
- Average Cost Basis: Total_Invested / Total_Holdings per instrument
- Cost Basis vs Current Price: how far above/below cost basis is current price
- DCA vs Lump Sum Comparison: what would the return be if all capital had been
  deployed at the start vs DCA'd in over time?
- Signal-Enhanced vs Vanilla DCA: what would the return be with fixed amounts?
  (This is the KEY metric — the signal enhancement must add value)

10.2 STRATEGY-SPECIFIC METRICS

- Signal Multiplier Distribution: histogram of multiplier values over time
- Fear & Greed Correlation: does buying more during fear produce better returns?
- RSI Correlation: does buying more when RSI is low produce better returns?
- Crash-Buy Performance: PnL on crash-buy purchases specifically
  (Track each crash-buy separately to evaluate its impact)
- Cost Basis Improvement: percentage by which signal-enhanced cost basis is
  better than vanilla DCA cost basis
- Monthly Spend Variance: how much does actual spending vary month to month?
- Accumulation Rate: units accumulated per dollar spent over time
- Time-Weighted Return: measures the investment manager's performance
  independent of cash flow timing

10.3 PORTFOLIO ANALYTICS

- Asset allocation breakdown (BTC %, ETH %, etc.)
- Unrealized PnL per instrument
- Historical cost basis chart overlaid on price chart
- Cumulative investment curve vs portfolio value curve

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 ALL SIGNALS SIMULTANEOUSLY EXTREME
- If RSI < 20, Fear & Greed < 10, price 30% below 200 SMA, and SOPR < 0.9:
  The multiplier could be very high (2.5-3.0x)
- This is BY DESIGN — buying aggressively during maximum fear is the core edge
- The monthly budget cap prevents unlimited spending
- The crash-buy budget provides additional firepower for these moments

11.2 SIGNALS CONFLICT
- RSI says oversold (buy more) but Fear & Greed says greed (buy less)
- The geometric mean naturally handles this: conflicting signals produce a
  near-neutral multiplier (close to 1.0)
- This is the desired behavior — when signals disagree, default to base amount

11.3 EXTENDED BEAR MARKET (12+ MONTHS)
- The strategy will continue buying throughout, potentially accumulating
  significant unrealized losses
- The emergency stop (Section 4.3) provides a safety net at 50% drawdown
- Historical evidence supports continued DCA through bear markets: every
  past bear market has eventually recovered and rewarded DCA investors

11.4 STABLECOIN DEPEG
- If USDT depegs from $1.00 (e.g., trades at $0.95), all USDT-denominated
  calculations are distorted
- Monitor USDT/USD price via external source
- If USDT depegs > 2%: halt all purchases and alert operator

11.5 EXCHANGE DELISTS AN INSTRUMENT
- If a DCA target instrument is announced for delisting:
  Halt purchases for that instrument
  Alert operator with current holdings
  Do NOT auto-sell (operator may want to withdraw to external wallet)

11.6 FEAR & GREED API DEPRECATION
- If the external API becomes permanently unavailable:
  Switch to internal sentiment proxy:
  Internal_FG = weighted average of (RSI normalization, volume change,
  social media volume via CryptoCompare if available, price momentum)
  This is a degraded but functional fallback
