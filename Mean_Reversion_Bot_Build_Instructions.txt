SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

This strategy bets that overextended single-asset prices will revert to their
statistical mean. When an asset's price deviates significantly from its moving
average (measured by Z-score, Bollinger Bands, or RSI extremes), the strategy
enters a counter-trend position expecting a snapback. It is designed as a
complement to STRAT-001 (trend following) — active during ranging markets
where trend following underperforms.

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- Range-bound, choppy, sideways markets
- Post-trend exhaustion — after strong moves when mean-reverting behavior is
  most likely
- Assets exhibiting Hurst exponent < 0.5 (confirmed mean-reverting dynamics)
- High-volatility but non-trending environments (whipsawing conditions)

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- Backtesting shows win rates of 55-70% for mean reversion (higher than
  momentum's 35-45%)
- Combining mean reversion with momentum reduces maximum drawdown from 29%
  to 20% while smoothing the equity curve
- Mean reversion capital is deployed only 3-7% of the time, making it a
  capital-efficient opportunistic strategy
- The edge exploits short-term overreaction by retail traders, temporary
  liquidity imbalances, and stop-loss cascade reversals

1.4 TARGET INSTRUMENTS

Primary: BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT perpetual futures
Secondary: SOL/USDT, BNB/USDT, XRP/USDT, ADA/USDT, AVAX/USDT, DOGE/USDT
Universe cap: Maximum 8 instruments
Only instruments with Hurst exponent < 0.5 on the analysis timeframe are eligible

1.5 TIMEFRAMES

Primary signal: 4-hour candles
Regime filter: Daily candles (Hurst exponent, ADX check)
Entry timing: 15-minute candles
Position monitoring: 1-minute candles

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 REGIME CLASSIFICATION (DAILY — RUN AT 00:00 UTC)

Before any trading signals are evaluated, classify the current market regime
for each instrument:

Step 1: Hurst Exponent Calculation
- Use 100 daily closes (rolling window)
- Method: Rescaled Range (R/S) analysis
- Classification:
  H < 0.40: Strong mean reversion — use AGGRESSIVE entry thresholds
  H 0.40-0.50: Moderate mean reversion — use STANDARD thresholds
  H 0.50-0.55: Random walk — PAUSE this instrument (neither trending nor reverting)
  H > 0.55: Trending — DISABLE mean reversion, defer to STRAT-001

Step 2: ADX Regime Check (Daily)
- ADX(14) on daily candles
- ADX < 20: Ranging market — IDEAL for mean reversion
- ADX 20-25: Transitional — use STANDARD thresholds, reduced size
- ADX > 25: Trending — DISABLE mean reversion for this instrument

Step 3: Combined Regime Decision
- Mean reversion is ACTIVE only if: Hurst < 0.50 AND ADX < 25
- If either condition fails, the instrument is excluded from the active
  universe until the next daily recalculation

3.2 PRIMARY ENTRY SIGNALS

Signal A: Bollinger Band Extreme
- Calculate Bollinger Bands on 4h candles: 20-period SMA ± 2.5 standard deviations
  (Note: 2.5σ, not standard 2.0σ, to reduce false signals in crypto)
- LONG signal: 4h candle closes BELOW the lower Bollinger Band
- SHORT signal: 4h candle closes ABOVE the upper Bollinger Band

Signal B: Z-Score Extreme
- Calculate 50-period rolling mean and standard deviation on 4h closes
- Z = (Close − Mean_50) / StdDev_50
- LONG signal: Z < −2.0
- SHORT signal: Z > +2.0

Signal C: RSI Extreme
- RSI(14) on 4h candles
- LONG signal: RSI drops below 25 (stricter than standard 30)
- SHORT signal: RSI rises above 75 (stricter than standard 70)

ENTRY REQUIREMENT: At least 2 of 3 signals must agree.
- If all 3 agree: Full position size (STRONG signal)
- If 2 of 3 agree: 75% position size (MODERATE signal)
- If only 1 signals: NO TRADE

3.3 REVERSAL CONFIRMATION (REQUIRED)

After the extreme is detected, do NOT enter immediately. Wait for evidence
that the reversal has begun:

Step 1: Wait for a 15-minute candle that closes in the reversal direction
- For LONG: a 15m candle that closes GREEN (close > open) after the extreme
- For SHORT: a 15m candle that closes RED (close < open) after the extreme

Step 2: Volume Confirmation on the Reversal Candle
- The reversal candle on 15m must have volume > 1.0x the 20-period average
  (at least average volume — proof of participation in the reversal)

Step 3: Order Book Imbalance Check
- At the moment of entry signal, check the depth20 order book:
  Buy_Depth = sum of bid quantities at top 10 levels
  Sell_Depth = sum of ask quantities at top 10 levels
  Imbalance_Ratio = Buy_Depth / Sell_Depth
- For LONG entry: Imbalance_Ratio must be > 1.2 (more buy pressure than sell)
- For SHORT entry: Imbalance_Ratio must be < 0.8 (more sell pressure than buy)
- If imbalance does not confirm: wait for next 15m candle (up to 3 candles)
- If no confirmation within 3 candles (45 minutes): cancel the signal

3.4 MAXIMUM WAIT TIME

- The extreme signal on 4h is valid for the duration of that 4h candle PLUS
  the next 4h candle (8 hours total)
- If confirmation (Section 3.3) does not occur within 8 hours, discard the
  signal entirely
- If price reverts more than 50% of the extreme move before entry, the
  opportunity is diminished — discard

3.5 ORDER PLACEMENT

- Order type: LIMIT at best bid (for longs) or best ask (for shorts)
- Timeout: 30 seconds. Cancel and re-place at current best bid/ask.
- After 3 failed LIMIT attempts: MARKET order
- Immediately after fill, place:
  STOP_MARKET for stop loss
  TAKE_PROFIT_MARKET for first target
- Log all entry details with millisecond timestamps

SECTION 4: EXIT LOGIC
================================================================================

4.1 MEAN REVERSION TARGET (PRIMARY EXIT)

- Target: Price returns to the 20-period SMA on the 4h timeframe (the center
  of the Bollinger Bands)
- This is the "mean" the strategy expects price to revert to
- For LONG positions: exit when price touches or exceeds the 20 SMA (4h)
- For SHORT positions: exit when price touches or drops below the 20 SMA (4h)

4.2 PARTIAL TAKE PROFIT SCALING

Tranche 1: Close 40% at 50% of the distance from entry to the 20 SMA
- Example: Entry at $58,000, 20 SMA at $60,000, distance = $2,000
  Close 40% at $59,000 (50% reversion)

Tranche 2: Close 30% when price reaches the 20 SMA (full mean reversion)

Tranche 3: Close remaining 30% at the opposite Bollinger Band
  (or Z-score ±1.0 on the opposite side, whichever comes first)
  This tranche captures occasional overshoots past the mean

4.3 HARD STOP LOSS

- Placement: 1.0 standard deviation beyond the extreme that triggered entry
  (i.e., if entry was at lower BB, stop is at lower BB − 1.0σ)
- In Z-score terms: if entry at Z = −2.0, stop at Z = −3.0
  (1.0σ additional beyond the entry extreme)
- Maximum dollar loss per trade: 1.5% of account equity
- Order type: STOP_MARKET on Binance (server-side)
- Placed within 500ms of entry fill

4.4 TIME-BASED EXIT

- Maximum holding period: 5 days (120 four-hour candles)
- This is derived from mean reversion's typical timescale
- If position has not reached the first take profit (50% reversion) within
  2.5 days: close 50% at market
- At 5 days: close entirely regardless of PnL

4.5 REGIME CHANGE EXIT

- If the daily regime classification (Section 3.1) changes from "mean-reverting"
  to "trending" (Hurst > 0.55 or ADX > 30) while a position is open:
  Close the position within 2 hours, regardless of current PnL
  Mean reversion trades in a trending regime are swimming against the tide

4.6 BREAKEVEN STOP

- When unrealized profit reaches 50% of the distance to the 20 SMA (first
  take profit level), move stop loss to entry price + fees
- This protects the position from turning negative after partial reversion

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 POSITION SIZING & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital for this strategy: 20% of total account equity
  (Lower than trend following because mean reversion has higher win rate but
   smaller average wins — catastrophic trend-captures must be contained)
- Maximum per trade: 4% of account equity
- Position sizing based on stop distance:
  Size = (Equity × 1.0%) / Stop_Distance_Percent
  (1.0% risk per trade — conservative for counter-trend trades)

5.2 STOP LOSS ARCHITECTURE

Hard stop: 1.0σ beyond entry extreme (approximately Z = −3.0 or +3.0)
Dollar cap: 1.5% of equity maximum (whichever is tighter)
Breakeven: At 50% reversion (Tranche 1 level)
Time stop: 5 days maximum

5.3 DRAWDOWN PROTECTION

Daily: 2% halt
Weekly: 4% halt
Monthly: 7% halt
System-wide: 15% (same)

5.4 EXPOSURE LIMITS

- Maximum concurrent positions: 3 (mean reversion is selective)
- Maximum single-asset exposure: 6% of equity
- Net directional exposure: 12% (less constrained because positions are
  short-lived and counter-trend)

5.5 ANTI-TREND SAFEGUARD

- This is the most important risk rule for mean reversion:
- If price moves 1.0σ beyond the stop loss level (Z reaches ±4.0), this
  strongly suggests a structural trend change, not a temporary extreme
- Emergency exit: close immediately at market, accept the loss
- Add the instrument to a "trending" blacklist for 7 days (no mean reversion)

5.6 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

Same structure as STRAT-001 Section 5.7, with addition:
- If 2 mean reversion trades in the same day hit stops, halt the strategy
  for 24 hours (market is trending, not mean-reverting)

SECTION 6: POSITION SIZING RULES
================================================================================

6.1 BASE CALCULATION

Position_Size = (Equity × Risk_Per_Trade) / Stop_Distance_Pct

Where:
- Risk_Per_Trade = 1.0% of equity (base)
- Stop_Distance_Pct = (Stop_Price − Entry_Price) / Entry_Price
  = approximately 1.0σ of the 4h distribution beyond the entry extreme

6.2 SIGNAL STRENGTH ADJUSTMENT

- STRONG signal (all 3 indicators agree): 100% of calculated size
- MODERATE signal (2 of 3 agree): 75% of calculated size
- Additional: if Hurst < 0.40 (strong mean reversion): +25% bonus to size
  (capped at maximum per-trade limit)

6.3 LEVERAGE

- Maximum leverage: 3x
- Preferred leverage: 2x
- If ATR/Price > 4% (high vol): reduce to 1x

6.4 COMPOUND GROWTH

- Same as STRAT-001 Section 6.4
- Equity-based sizing automatically compounds

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 REGIME FILTER (MOST IMPORTANT)
- Hurst exponent must be < 0.50 AND ADX < 25
- Checked daily, overrides ALL signals if not met

7.2 TREND FOLLOWING CONFLICT
- If STRAT-001 (trend following) has an active position in the SAME instrument
  in the SAME direction as the trend, do NOT enter a counter-trend mean
  reversion trade
- Specifically: if STRAT-001 is LONG BTC and mean reversion detects a SHORT
  signal on BTC (overbought RSI > 75), REJECT the mean reversion short
- Mean reversion may trade instruments that STRAT-001 is NOT currently trading

7.3 WHIPSAW PROTECTION
- Minimum 6 hours between trades on the same instrument
- After a stop loss, minimum 12 hours before re-entry on that instrument

7.4 EXTREME VOLATILITY FILTER
- If 4h ATR exceeds 2x its 50-period average, mean reversion signals are
  SUSPECT — the market is in a volatility expansion that favors trends
- In this condition: increase entry threshold from 2.0σ to 2.5σ, and reduce
  position size by 50%

7.5 FEE THRESHOLD
- Expected profit (distance from entry to 20 SMA) must exceed 3x round-trip
  fees (0.08% × 3 = 0.24% minimum expected move)
- If the 20 SMA is too close to the current price, the trade is not viable

7.6 CONSECUTIVE LOSS HANDLING
- After 3 consecutive losses: reduce size by 25% for 3 trades
- After 5 consecutive losses: halt strategy, review regime classification logic
- After halt: require 5 days of paper trading confirmation before resuming

7.7 LIQUIDITY FILTER
- Same as STRAT-001 Section 7.1 (spread < 0.1%, adequate depth)

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 TREND RESUMES AFTER APPARENT REVERSION
- Price reverts 50% (triggers Tranche 1 and breakeven stop) then resumes the
  trend, stopping out the remaining position at breakeven
- This is a NORMAL outcome, not a failure. The partial take profit secured gains.
- Log and track frequency: if this happens > 40% of trades, the entry timing
  may need adjustment

11.2 EXTREME DOES NOT REVERT (REGIME SHIFT MID-TRADE)
- The 4h extreme continues extending (Z reaches −3.0, −4.0, −5.0)
- The hard stop at Z = −3.0 catches this
- Post-stop: daily regime recalculation will likely reclassify the instrument
- This is the primary risk of mean reversion — trends disguised as extremes

11.3 BOLLINGER BANDS COMPRESS (LOW VOLATILITY)
- When bands narrow, the 2.5σ threshold becomes a very small price move
- The FEE THRESHOLD filter (Section 7.5) prevents entering trades where the
  expected reversion is too small to cover costs
- If BB width (as % of price) drops below 2%, skip signals on that instrument

11.4 FALSE REVERSAL CONFIRMATION
- 15m candle shows reversal, but it's a dead-cat bounce
- The breakeven stop protects against this after Tranche 1
- The time stop (5 days) prevents indefinite holding
- Track false confirmation rate; if > 50%, tighten the confirmation criteria

11.5 ALL INSTRUMENTS CLASSIFIED AS TRENDING
- If daily regime check classifies ALL instruments as trending (all ADX > 25
  or all Hurst > 0.5), the strategy has zero eligible instruments
- This is expected behavior during strong bull/bear phases
- The strategy sits idle, preserving capital. This is a feature, not a bug.
- Log idle periods for performance analysis
