SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

Market making continuously places simultaneous buy (bid) and sell (ask) limit
orders around the mid-price of a crypto asset, earning the bid-ask spread as
profit. The strategy acts as a liquidity provider — when other traders hit
the bid or lift the ask, the market maker captures the spread. The core
challenge is managing inventory risk (accumulating too much of one side) while
maintaining competitive quotes.

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- High-volume, moderately volatile markets with consistent two-way flow
- LESS liquid pairs where the spread is wide enough to generate meaningful
  profit after fees (major pairs like BTC/USDT have spreads too tight for
  retail market makers — professional firms with co-located servers dominate)
- Mid-tier altcoin futures pairs with $30-200M daily volume

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- The Avellaneda-Stoikov model provides the mathematical foundation for
  optimal quote placement and inventory management
- Professional market makers target 10-30% annually with Sharpe > 2.0
- Retail market making via Hummingbot on mid-tier tokens can generate 5-50%
  annually, but with significant inventory risk during directional moves
- The edge comes from providing liquidity (a service) and being compensated
  via the spread — it is a TIME-based edge, not a directional one

1.4 TARGET INSTRUMENTS

DO NOT market-make on BTC/USDT or ETH/USDT perpetuals — spreads are too
tight and professional firms will outcompete.

Target: Mid-tier perpetual futures with characteristics:
- 24h volume: $30M - $200M (enough liquidity for fills, not enough for
  professional firms to fully dominate)
- Average spread: > 0.03% (must exceed 2× maker fee to be viable)
- Examples: DOGE/USDT, DOT/USDT, NEAR/USDT, FTM/USDT, ATOM/USDT, APE/USDT,
  SAND/USDT, MANA/USDT, and similar mid-cap tokens

Maximum concurrent instruments: 3
Selection criteria: widest profitable spread with adequate volume

1.5 MATHEMATICAL FOUNDATION — AVELLANEDA-STOIKOV MODEL

The model provides the optimal reservation (fair) price and optimal spread:

Reservation Price: r(s, q, t) = s − q × γ × σ² × (T − t)

Where:
- s = current mid-price
- q = current inventory (positive if long, negative if short)
- γ = risk aversion parameter (higher = more aggressive inventory reduction)
- σ² = variance of price (volatility squared)
- T = end of trading session (in crypto, use rolling 8-hour windows)
- t = current time

Optimal Spread: δ = γ × σ² × (T − t) + (2/γ) × ln(1 + γ/κ)

Where:
- κ = order book depth parameter (higher = deeper book = wider spread needed)

The reservation price shifts quotes to manage inventory:
- If holding excess LONG inventory (q > 0): reservation price drops below
  mid-price, making the ask more competitive (encouraging sells to reduce
  inventory) and the bid less competitive
- If holding excess SHORT inventory (q < 0): opposite shift

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 MODEL PARAMETER CALIBRATION (RUN ON STARTUP AND EVERY HOUR)

Step 1: Volatility Estimation (σ)
- Calculate realized volatility from 1-minute candle returns over the last
  60 minutes: σ = stdev(ln(Close_i / Close_(i-1))) × sqrt(60)
- This gives hourly volatility
- Annualize: σ_annual = σ_hourly × sqrt(8760)
- Update every 15 minutes using a 60-minute rolling window

Step 2: Order Book Depth Parameter (κ)
- Measure the order book intensity: average depth (in USDT) at the best 5
  levels on both sides over the last 30 minutes
- κ = Average_Depth / (Mid_Price × 0.001)
  This normalizes depth relative to a 0.1% price move
- Higher κ = deeper book = market maker can use tighter spread
- Lower κ = thinner book = wider spread needed

Step 3: Risk Aversion Parameter (γ)
- Base γ = 0.1 (moderate risk aversion)
- Adjust based on current inventory:
  If |inventory| > 50% of max allowed: γ = 0.3 (aggressive inventory reduction)
  If |inventory| > 75% of max allowed: γ = 0.5 (very aggressive)
  If |inventory| < 25% of max: γ = 0.05 (relaxed, accumulate spread income)

Step 4: Session Time Parameter (T − t)
- Use rolling 8-hour windows aligned with funding timestamps
- T − t = time remaining until next funding timestamp (in fractional hours)
- As T − t approaches 0, the model naturally widens spreads (less time to
  recover from adverse fills)

3.2 QUOTE CALCULATION

Every time the model parameters or market data update meaningfully (at least
every 5 seconds, more frequently during active markets):

Step 1: Calculate Reservation Price
- r = mid_price − inventory × γ × σ² × (T − t)
- mid_price = (best_bid + best_ask) / 2 from bookTicker

Step 2: Calculate Optimal Half-Spread
- half_spread = (γ × σ² × (T − t) + (2/γ) × ln(1 + γ/κ)) / 2

Step 3: Determine Quote Prices
- bid_quote = r − half_spread
- ask_quote = r + half_spread

Step 4: Round to Tick Size
- Round bid_quote DOWN to the nearest tick (price precision for the instrument)
- Round ask_quote UP to the nearest tick
- This ensures the quotes are valid prices on Binance

Step 5: Minimum Spread Enforcement
- The spread (ask_quote − bid_quote) must be at least:
  Minimum_Spread = 2 × maker_fee + 0.005% (safety margin)
  = 2 × 0.02% + 0.005% = 0.045%
- If calculated spread is tighter, widen symmetrically until minimum is met

Step 6: Maximum Spread Enforcement
- Maximum_Spread = 5 × current market spread (avoid being too far from market)
- If calculated spread exceeds this, narrow to Maximum_Spread

3.3 QUOTE PLACEMENT AND MANAGEMENT

Step 7: Place/Update Quotes
- If no quotes are currently active: place bid and ask limit orders
- If quotes exist: check if new calculated prices differ from current orders
  by more than 0.01% of price
  If YES: cancel old orders, place new ones (cancel-replace cycle)
  If NO: leave existing orders (avoid unnecessary API consumption)

Step 8: Quote Sizing
- Each quote (bid and ask) has a quantity determined by Section 6
- Bid size and ask size may differ based on inventory:
  If LONG inventory: ask size = 1.5× base size, bid size = 0.5× base size
  (encourage sells, discourage buys — inventory reduction)
  If SHORT inventory: opposite
  If neutral: equal sizes

Step 9: Quote Layering (Optional, Advanced)
- Place quotes at multiple levels, not just one:
  Level 1 (tight): 40% of size at calculated optimal prices
  Level 2 (wider): 35% of size at 1.5× half_spread from reservation price
  Level 3 (widest): 25% of size at 2× half_spread from reservation price
- This provides depth and captures fills even during wider price swings
- But consumes more API rate limit budget — only enable if budget allows

3.4 FILL HANDLING

When a BID fills (someone sold to us — we accumulated inventory):
- Update inventory count immediately
- Recalculate reservation price (it will shift downward for longs)
- Update or replace the ask quote (it becomes more competitive to offload)
- Log: fill price, mid-price at fill time, fill size, inventory delta

When an ASK fills (someone bought from us — we reduced inventory):
- Update inventory count immediately
- Recalculate reservation price
- Update or replace the bid quote
- Log: fill price, mid-price at fill time, spread captured, inventory delta

Completed round-trip (buy fill matched with subsequent sell):
- Calculate gross profit: ask_fill − bid_fill
- Calculate net profit: gross − maker_fee_bid − maker_fee_ask
- Credit to strategy PnL

SECTION 4: EXIT LOGIC
================================================================================

4.1 QUOTE WITHDRAWAL (TEMPORARY EXIT)

The strategy exits the market (cancels all quotes) under these conditions:
- Volatility spike: if 1-minute realized vol exceeds 3× the hourly average,
  withdraw all quotes for 5 minutes (spread becomes too uncertain)
- Large order detected: if aggTrade shows a single trade > 5× average trade
  size, withdraw for 30 seconds (potential informed trader)
- Spread collapse: if the market spread narrows below the minimum viable
  spread (Section 3.2 Step 5), withdraw until spread widens

4.2 INVENTORY LIQUIDATION

If inventory accumulates beyond the maximum threshold (Section 5.3):
- Phase 1: Skew quotes aggressively (γ increase, Section 3.1 Step 3)
- Phase 2: If inventory still excessive after 30 minutes, place a MARKET
  order to reduce inventory by 50%
- Phase 3: If inventory exceeds emergency threshold, close ALL inventory
  at market and pause strategy for 1 hour

4.3 END OF SESSION

- Since crypto markets are 24/7, there is no session end
- Instead, at each 8-hour funding timestamp, perform a soft reset:
  Cancel all quotes
  If holding any inventory, evaluate: is the carry (funding rate) favorable?
  If yes: hold through funding payment, then resume quoting
  If no: reduce inventory to zero, then resume quoting
  This prevents paying negative funding on accumulated inventory

4.4 STRATEGY HALT

- If daily PnL is negative (market making should be consistently profitable
  in small amounts), halt and investigate
- A negative day suggests adverse selection (informed traders picking off
  stale quotes) or a regime change (trending market chewing through one side)

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 POSITION SIZING & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital for this strategy: 15% of total account equity
- Maximum per instrument: 7% of total equity
- The capital must be viewed as MARGIN for inventory, not as deployment target
  (the goal is to earn spread, not deploy capital directionally)

5.2 LEVERAGE

- Maximum leverage: 3x
- Preferred: 2x
- Leverage is needed because quoted sizes should be meaningful relative to
  the order book, but actual capital at risk is much less than notional

5.3 INVENTORY LIMITS

- Maximum LONG inventory: 3% of total account equity (notional)
- Maximum SHORT inventory: 3% of total account equity (notional)
- If inventory reaches 2%: increase γ to aggressive (0.3)
- If inventory reaches 3%: stop placing new orders on the accumulating side
- If inventory reaches 4% (post-fill overshoot): market order to reduce to 2%

5.4 DRAWDOWN PROTECTION

Daily drawdown limit: 1.0% (very tight — market making should not have
large drawdowns)
Weekly: 2%
Monthly: 4%
System-wide: 15%

5.5 ADVERSE SELECTION DETECTION

- Track the percentage of fills that are immediately profitable vs unprofitable:
  After each fill, measure mid-price 1 second later
  If mid-price moved AGAINST the fill direction > 60% of the time, the
  strategy is being adversely selected (informed traders are picking it off)
- If adverse selection rate exceeds 60% over 100 fills: halt and widen spreads
  by 50% before resuming
- If adverse selection rate exceeds 70%: halt the strategy on that instrument

5.6 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

Same structure as previous strategies, plus:
- Quote staleness: if both bid and ask have been live for > 60 seconds without
  an update, check if the system is healthy (potential bug or frozen state)
- Inventory blowout: if inventory swings from max LONG to max SHORT (or vice
  versa) within 1 hour, halt — this suggests the market is whipsawing through
  the quotes too aggressively

Kill Switch:
- Cancel all orders on all instruments
- Liquidate all inventory
- Must complete within 10 seconds

SECTION 6: POSITION SIZING RULES
================================================================================

6.1 QUOTE SIZE CALCULATION

Base_Quote_Size = (Allocated_Capital × 0.2) / Current_Price

This means each quote represents about 20% of allocated capital — with bids
and asks alternating, the maximum inventory if ALL bids fill is approximately
the allocated capital.

6.2 INVENTORY-ADJUSTED SIZING (from Section 3.3 Step 8)

When LONG inventory:
  Bid_Size = Base_Quote_Size × (1 − inventory_pct × 2)  [min 0.1× base]
  Ask_Size = Base_Quote_Size × (1 + inventory_pct × 2)  [max 2× base]

When SHORT inventory: mirror the above

inventory_pct = abs(current_inventory_notional) / max_inventory_notional

6.3 MINIMUM ORDER SIZE

- All quote orders must meet Binance minimum notional
- If the calculated size falls below minimum: increase to minimum
- If increasing to minimum would breach inventory limits: skip the order

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 TREND FILTER
- If 1h EMA(20) is trending strongly (> 0.1% per hour slope), widen spreads
  by 25% (trending markets cause systematic adverse selection on one side)
- If daily ADX > 30: halt market making on that instrument

7.2 SPREAD VIABILITY
- If the natural market spread (best ask − best bid) / mid_price is below
  0.03%: the instrument is too tightly traded for retail market making
  Skip this instrument

7.3 VOLUME FILTER
- 24h volume must be between $30M and $200M
- Below $30M: fills will be too infrequent
- Above $200M: professional firms dominate, spreads too tight

7.4 LIQUIDATION CASCADE FILTER
- If forceOrder stream shows elevated liquidation activity (> 10 events per
  minute): withdraw all quotes for 10 minutes

7.5 FEE THRESHOLD
- Minimum spread after model calculation must exceed 2× round-trip maker fees
  Minimum: 2 × (0.02% + 0.02%) = 0.08%
- If model suggests tighter spread: do NOT go below 0.08%

7.6 NEWS/EVENT FILTER
- During high-impact events: withdraw all quotes 1 hour before, resume
  30 minutes after
- Spreads widen dramatically during events; the risk of adverse selection
  is extremely high

SECTION 9: PAPER TRADING SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

9.1 FILL SIMULATION

- LIMIT bid fills: execute when aggTrade price ≤ bid_price AND volume > 0
- LIMIT ask fills: execute when aggTrade price ≥ ask_price AND volume > 0
- Simulate queue position: if our quote is at the same price as existing
  depth, only fill proportional to our size vs total depth at that level
- This is CRITICAL for market making simulation — assuming immediate fills
  at limit price dramatically overestimates profitability

9.2 ADVERSE SELECTION SIMULATION

- After each simulated fill, track 1-second forward price movement
- This determines whether the simulated fills were truly profitable or
  whether they would have been adversely selected in live trading

9.3 GO-LIVE CRITERIA (30-day minimum)

- Net positive PnL after simulated fees
- Profit per day is positive on > 70% of days
- Adverse selection rate < 55%
- Maximum inventory drawdown < 2%
- Sharpe ratio > 1.5 (market making should be high-Sharpe)
- Average spread captured > 2× maker fees

SECTION 10: PERFORMANCE METRICS

10.1 STANDARD METRICS — Same as STRAT-001

10.2 STRATEGY-SPECIFIC METRICS

- Spread Captured Per Fill: average (fill_price − mid_price_at_fill)
- Inventory Turnover: how many times the inventory cycles per day
- Quote Uptime: percentage of time both bid and ask are active
- Fill Rate: percentage of quotes that result in fills
- Adverse Selection Rate: percentage of fills where mid-price moves against
  within 1 second
- Realized Spread vs Quoted Spread: how much of the quoted spread is actually
  captured after adverse selection
- Inventory PnL vs Spread PnL: decompose total PnL into spread income
  (good) and inventory mark-to-market (risky)
- Order-to-Fill Ratio: number of orders placed per fill (efficiency metric)
- API Efficiency: rate limit budget consumed per dollar of profit

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 QUOTE STUFFING / WASH TRADING ON THE INSTRUMENT
- If detected (unusual order/cancel patterns in depth stream), widen spreads
  and reduce quote sizes

11.2 SUDDEN LIQUIDITY WITHDRAWAL
- If order book depth at top 5 levels drops below 50% of normal within 5
  seconds, withdraw all quotes immediately (potential flash crash setup)

11.3 BOTH QUOTES FILL IN RAPID SUCCESSION (INVENTORY SWING)
- Bid fills followed immediately by ask fill (or vice versa) = profitable
  double-fill, ideal scenario
- Bid fills followed by ANOTHER bid fill before ask fills = rapid inventory
  accumulation, potentially problematic
- If 3+ fills on the same side occur within 10 seconds without any fills
  on the opposite side: withdraw the accumulating side's quote, keep the
  reducing side's quote active

11.4 MAKER FEE CHANGE OR LOSS OF MAKER STATUS
- If account loses maker fee tier (due to volume changes), recalculate
  minimum viable spread
- If maker fee increases by > 50%: halt and re-evaluate instrument viability

11.5 INSTRUMENT DELISTING ANNOUNCEMENT
- If an instrument is announced for delisting: immediately close all
  inventory and cease quoting on that instrument
- Liquidity will evaporate rapidly after delisting announcements
