SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

This strategy exploits price discrepancies across different Binance trading
venues and between related trading pairs. It operates in two modes:

Mode A — Cross-Exchange Arbitrage (Spot vs Futures):
Buy on Binance spot and sell on Binance futures (or vice versa) when a
price discrepancy exceeds transaction costs. This is intra-exchange arb
between spot and derivatives markets.

Mode B — Triangular Arbitrage:
Exploit pricing inconsistencies between three trading pairs on a single
exchange. Example: BTC/USDT → ETH/BTC → ETH/USDT → USDT. If the cross-rate
differs from the direct rate, a profit opportunity exists.

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- High volatility moments when prices across venues temporarily desynchronize
- Periods of high demand imbalance (heavy buying on one venue, not the other)
- During large liquidation events where derivatives prices overshoot spot
- After major news breaks when information propagates unevenly across pairs

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- Price discrepancies are brief (milliseconds to seconds) but persistent
  due to crypto market fragmentation
- Typical opportunities: 0.1-2% per trade on altcoins, much less on majors
- Triangular opportunities: 0.05-0.5% gross, diminished by triple fees
- The edge requires SPEED — opportunities are competed away within seconds
- Realistic after fees: many opportunities yield < 0.05% net, requiring high
  volume and low fees to generate meaningful returns

1.4 TARGET OPPORTUNITIES

Mode A — Spot-Futures Discrepancy:
Primary: BTC, ETH, SOL, BNB, XRP, DOGE, ADA
Monitor spot price vs futures mark price continuously
Opportunity: When |spot_price − futures_price| / spot_price > threshold

Mode B — Triangular Paths:
Path 1: USDT → BTC → ETH → USDT
Path 2: USDT → BTC → SOL → USDT
Path 3: USDT → ETH → BNB → USDT
Path 4: USDT → BTC → XRP → USDT
Path 5-10: Additional triangles involving top 10 assets

Maximum monitored triangles: 10 paths
Maximum concurrent executions: 2 (to avoid leg-timing conflicts)

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 MODE A — SPOT-FUTURES ARBITRAGE DETECTION

Step 1: Continuous Price Comparison
- For each monitored asset, compare in real-time:
  Spot_Best_Bid, Spot_Best_Ask (from spot bookTicker)
  Futures_Best_Bid, Futures_Best_Ask (from futures bookTicker)
- Update on every bookTicker message (sub-second frequency)

Step 2: Opportunity Calculation
Opportunity 1 — Buy Spot, Sell Futures (when futures premium is excessive):
  Gross_Profit_Pct = (Futures_Best_Bid − Spot_Best_Ask) / Spot_Best_Ask × 100
  Net_Profit_Pct = Gross_Profit_Pct − Spot_Taker_Fee − Futures_Taker_Fee
  (All arb trades use MARKET/taker execution for speed)

Opportunity 2 — Buy Futures, Sell Spot (when futures discount is excessive):
  Gross_Profit_Pct = (Spot_Best_Bid − Futures_Best_Ask) / Futures_Best_Ask × 100
  Net_Profit_Pct = Gross_Profit_Pct − Spot_Taker_Fee − Futures_Taker_Fee

Step 3: Threshold Check
- Minimum Net_Profit_Pct to execute: 0.05% (after fees)
- Conservative threshold: 0.10%
- Default: 0.08%
- This threshold must be recalibrated weekly based on average fee tier

Step 4: Liquidity Verification
- Check depth5 on both venues: is there sufficient quantity at the best
  bid/ask to fill the intended order size?
- Required available depth: order_size × 1.5 (50% buffer for partial fills)
- If insufficient depth on either side: SKIP (slippage will eat the profit)

Step 5: Execution (if all conditions met)
- Both orders placed within 50ms of each other (tighter than other strategies
  because arb opportunities evaporate instantly)
- Both orders: MARKET type (speed over price improvement)
- If one order fills and the other fails: see Section 11.1

3.2 MODE B — TRIANGULAR ARBITRAGE DETECTION

Step 1: Continuous Cross-Rate Calculation
For triangle USDT → A → B → USDT:
- Direct rate: Buy B with USDT at B/USDT ask price
- Cross rate: Buy A with USDT at A/USDT ask, then sell A for B at B/A bid,
  then sell B for USDT at B/USDT bid

  Implied_Price = (A_USDT_Ask) / (B_A_Bid) vs B_USDT_Bid

Step 2: Profit Calculation
Starting with 1 USDT:
  Step A: Buy Asset_A → receive (1 / A_USDT_Ask) units of A
  Step B: Sell A for B → receive (A_qty × B_A_Bid) units of B
  Step C: Sell B for USDT → receive (B_qty × B_USDT_Bid) USDT

  Gross_Profit = Final_USDT − 1.0
  Net_Profit = Gross_Profit − (3 × Taker_Fee × Amount_Per_Leg)
  (Three trades, each incurring taker fee)

Step 3: Threshold Check
- Minimum Net_Profit_Pct: 0.05% after 3× taker fees
- With 0.10% taker per trade × 3 = 0.30% total fees, the gross opportunity
  must exceed 0.35% (0.30% + 0.05% minimum net)
- If fee tier is lower (maker taker 0.04%), threshold adjusts accordingly

Step 4: Execution Sequence
- All THREE orders must execute in rapid sequence
- Order 1: MARKET (placed immediately on detection)
- Order 2: MARKET (placed within 100ms of Order 1 fill confirmation)
- Order 3: MARKET (placed within 100ms of Order 2 fill confirmation)
- Total execution window target: < 500ms for all three orders
- Alternatively, if all three can be calculated in advance:
  Submit all three as quickly as possible, accepting that Order 2 and 3
  may need quantity adjustment based on actual fills

Step 5: Fill Reconciliation
- After all three orders fill, calculate actual net profit:
  Actual_Profit = Final_USDT − Initial_USDT − Total_Fees
- If actual profit is positive: success, log and continue
- If actual profit is negative (slippage exceeded expected profit):
  Log as a loss, analyze which leg had unexpected slippage

SECTION 4: EXIT LOGIC
================================================================================

4.1 ARBITRAGE IS SELF-CLOSING

Unlike other strategies, arbitrage trades are IMMEDIATELY closed by design.
Each trade is a complete round-trip (buy and sell simultaneously for Mode A,
or three-leg cycle for Mode B). There is no "holding period" in the
traditional sense.

4.2 STUCK POSITION HANDLING (THE REAL EXIT LOGIC)

If one leg of an arb trade fails and leaves an unhedged position:
- Time limit: the exposed position must be closed within 60 seconds
- If within 60 seconds the counter-opportunity appears (the price discrepancy
  reverses), execute the closing leg to complete the arb at a potentially
  smaller profit
- If no counter-opportunity within 30 seconds: close the position at market,
  accepting any profit or loss
- NEVER hold an unhedged arb position for more than 60 seconds

4.3 END-OF-DAY RECONCILIATION (00:00 UTC)

- Verify zero net inventory across all arb instruments
- If any residual position exists from failed arb legs: close at market
- Calculate daily arb P&L net of all fees

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 POSITION SIZING & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital for this strategy: 15% of total account equity
- Capital must be PRE-POSITIONED in both spot and futures wallets:
  Spot wallet: 50% of allocated capital
  Futures wallet: 50% of allocated capital
- Mode A and Mode B share the allocation

5.2 MAXIMUM TRADE SIZE

- Per arb trade (Mode A): maximum 2% of total equity per leg
- Per triangle (Mode B): maximum 1% of total equity per leg
  (Smaller because three sequential executions have higher failure risk)

5.3 EXPOSURE LIMITS

- Maximum unhedged directional exposure at any time: 2% of equity
  (Should normally be 0% — unhedged only during execution and failures)
- If unhedged exposure exceeds 2%: EMERGENCY close at market
- Maximum concurrent arb executions: 2 (avoid rate limit exhaustion)

5.4 DRAWDOWN PROTECTION

Daily: 1.5% halt (arb should have very low drawdowns)
Weekly: 2.5%
Monthly: 4%
System-wide: 15%

5.5 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

Execution Failure Rate:
- If more than 3 arb attempts in a row result in only 1 leg filling (the
  other failing): halt for 30 minutes (likely API or liquidity issue)

Negative Arb Trades:
- If 5 consecutive arb trades are net negative after fees: halt and review
  (fee calculations may be wrong, or latency is too high to capture the edge)

Exchange Anomaly:
- If bookTicker data shows prices that are clearly erroneous (> 5% deviation
  from recent history without corresponding market moves): DO NOT arb
  This could be a data feed error, and executing would expose to the correct
  price when the error resolves

Kill Switch:
- Cancel all pending orders, close any unhedged positions
- Must complete within 5 seconds

SECTION 6: POSITION SIZING RULES
================================================================================

6.1 PROFIT-MAXIMIZING SIZE

The optimal trade size is the MAXIMUM that can be filled at the quoted prices
without slipping to the next price level:

Mode A:
  Trade_Size = min(
    Spot_Best_Level_Quantity,
    Futures_Best_Level_Quantity,
    Max_Per_Trade_USDT / Current_Price,
    Available_Balance / Current_Price
  )

Mode B:
  Trade_Size = min(
    Quantities available at best price across all 3 legs,
    Max_Per_Trade_USDT / Current_Price,
    Available_Balance / 3 / Current_Price  (capital split across 3 legs)
  )

6.2 MINIMUM VIABLE SIZE

- Each arb trade must generate at least $0.50 net profit after all fees
  to justify the execution overhead
- Minimum_Size = $0.50 / Net_Profit_Percentage
  Example: 0.08% net profit → minimum size = $0.50 / 0.0008 = $625

6.3 LEVERAGE

- Mode A (spot vs futures): No leverage on spot leg. Max 2x on futures leg.
- Mode B (triangular, spot only): No leverage.

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 LATENCY FILTER
- Measure round-trip API latency every 60 seconds (REST order + confirmation)
- If latency exceeds 200ms: reduce arb activity (opportunities may be stale
  by the time orders arrive)
- If latency exceeds 500ms: halt arb completely (high risk of executing on
  stale prices)

7.2 DEPTH FILTER
- Both sides of Mode A (or all three legs of Mode B) must have depth ≥ 1.5x
  intended trade size at the best price level
- Without this, slippage will destroy the thin arb profit margin

7.3 FEE AWARENESS
- Recalculate fee thresholds on startup and when fee tier changes
- If BNB balance is sufficient for fee discount, factor in the reduced rate
- If BNB balance is insufficient: adjust thresholds to non-discounted rates

7.4 HIGH VOLATILITY CAUTION
- During extreme volatility (BTC moves > 5% in 15 minutes), arb spreads may
  appear wide but book depth is thin and fills are unreliable
- In extreme vol: increase minimum profit threshold by 2x

7.5 MAINTENANCE WINDOW
- If Binance announces maintenance for either spot or futures: halt arb
  2 hours before scheduled maintenance

7.6 CONSECUTIVE LOSS HANDLING
- After 5 net-negative arb trades: halt for 1 hour
- After 10 in a day: halt for remainder of day
- After 3 consecutive losing days: halt and require manual investigation

SECTION 9: PAPER TRADING SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

9.1 FILL SIMULATION

- Mode A: Simulate fills at best bid/ask PLUS slippage based on order size
  vs available depth. Walk the order book.
- Mode B: Simulate sequential fills with 100-200ms delays between legs.
  Each subsequent leg uses the CURRENT price (which may have moved since
  detection).

9.2 LATENCY SIMULATION

- Add 50-200ms random latency to each simulated order (models real API latency)
- The opportunity pricing may shift during this latency window

9.3 GO-LIVE CRITERIA (45-day minimum)

- Net positive PnL after all simulated fees
- Win rate > 80% (arb should have very high win rate)
- Average profit per trade > 0.03% net
- No unhedged position held > 60 seconds (failure handling works)
- Latency measurements show consistent sub-200ms round-trip

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 SINGLE LEG FILL (MODE A)
- Scenario: Spot buy fills, futures sell fails
- The bot now has an unhedged long spot position
- Response: Retry futures sell 3 times (200ms apart)
- If still failing: place futures sell as aggressive LIMIT (best bid - 0.01%)
- If still failing after 30 seconds: sell spot at market (unwinding)
- Maximum unhedged exposure time: 60 seconds (HARD LIMIT)

11.2 PARTIAL TRIANGLE (MODE B)
- Scenario: Legs 1 and 2 fill, Leg 3 fails
- The bot holds an intermediate asset that must be closed out
- Response: Retry Leg 3 three times (200ms apart)
- If failing: close the position by reversing Legs 1 and 2 (sell back through
  the original path) — this will likely result in a small loss
- NEVER hold incomplete triangle positions

11.3 ERRONEOUS PRICE DATA
- If a bookTicker reports a price that is > 2% from the last known price
  without corresponding aggTrade volume: suspect data error
- Do NOT execute arb based on the anomalous price
- Wait for 3 consecutive bookTicker updates confirming the new price level

11.4 RACE CONDITION WITH OTHER STRATEGIES
- If STRAT-001 places a MARKET buy on BTC at the same instant this strategy
  tries to arb-sell BTC, they may interfere
- The shared risk manager must coordinate: arb trades are ATOMIC and should
  not be split or delayed by other strategy orders

11.5 FEE TIER CHANGE MID-SESSION
- If a large fill changes the account's 30-day volume (affecting fee tier):
  immediately recalculate all thresholds
- If fees increased: wider threshold required, some opportunities become invalid
