SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

This strategy identifies pairs of cryptocurrencies that share a long-run
statistical equilibrium (cointegration), then trades temporary divergences
from that equilibrium. When the spread between the pair deviates significantly
from its mean, the strategy goes long the underperformer and short the
outperformer, profiting when the spread reverts to its historical average.

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- Range-bound or low-trend environments where pair spreads oscillate
- Periods where related crypto assets temporarily decouple due to
  idiosyncratic events (one asset dumps while the other holds)
- Works in BOTH bull and bear markets because it is market-neutral
  (long one asset, short another)

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- Cointegrated pairs have a mathematically provable tendency to revert to
  equilibrium, modeled by the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process
- Academic study (2022-2024): BTC-ETH pairs trading achieved 16.34%
  annualized returns with a Sharpe ratio of 2.45
- The edge is statistical and structural — it exploits temporary dislocations
  caused by asynchronous information processing, differential liquidity, and
  cross-exchange fragmentation

1.4 TARGET PAIR UNIVERSE

The strategy maintains a screened universe of cointegrated pairs. Initial
candidates (to be validated by cointegration testing):

Tier 1 (Highest expected cointegration):
- BTC/USDT vs ETH/USDT
- ETH/USDT vs SOL/USDT
- BTC/USDT vs BNB/USDT

Tier 2 (Sector pairs):
- SOL/USDT vs AVAX/USDT (L1 competitors)
- LINK/USDT vs DOT/USDT (infrastructure)
- MATIC/USDT vs ARB/USDT (L2 scaling)
- DOGE/USDT vs SHIB/USDT (meme sector)

Tier 3 (Expanded universe — test before trading):
- Any pair combination from top 30 crypto assets by market cap

Maximum active pairs simultaneously: 10
All trading on Binance USDT-margined perpetual futures

1.5 TIMEFRAMES

Primary analysis: 1-hour candles for spread calculation and signal generation
Cointegration testing: Daily candles, 180-day rolling window
Entry timing: 5-minute candles for execution refinement
Monitoring: 1-minute candles for active position management

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 COINTEGRATION TESTING — PAIR QUALIFICATION

This is the foundational analysis that determines WHICH pairs are eligible
for trading. Run daily at 00:00 UTC.

Step 1: Data Preparation
- For each candidate pair (Asset A, Asset B), fetch 180 days of daily
  close prices
- Calculate log prices: ln(Price_A), ln(Price_B)

Step 2: Engle-Granger Cointegration Test
- Run OLS regression: ln(Price_A) = α + β × ln(Price_B) + ε
- Store the hedge ratio β (this is the ratio of quantities to trade)
- Calculate residuals: Spread = ln(Price_A) − β × ln(Price_B)
- Run Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) test on the residuals:
  H0: Residuals have a unit root (no cointegration)
  H1: Residuals are stationary (cointegration exists)
- QUALIFICATION CRITERIA: ADF p-value < 0.05 (reject unit root at 5% level)
  Prefer pairs with p-value < 0.01 for higher confidence

Step 3: Half-Life Calculation
- Fit an AR(1) model to the spread: ΔSpread_t = θ × (Spread_t-1 − μ) + ε
- Half-life = −ln(2) / ln(1 + θ)
- QUALIFICATION CRITERIA: Half-life between 3 and 30 days
  Too short (< 3 days): Transaction costs eat profits
  Too long (> 30 days): Capital tied up unproductively

Step 4: Hurst Exponent Check
- Calculate Hurst exponent on the spread using rescaled range (R/S) analysis
- QUALIFICATION CRITERIA: H < 0.5 (confirms mean-reverting spread)
  Ideal: H < 0.4
  Marginal: H between 0.4 and 0.5 (trade with reduced size)
  Reject: H >= 0.5 (random walk or trending spread)

Step 5: Correlation Stability Check
- Calculate 30-day rolling correlation between Asset A and Asset B daily returns
- QUALIFICATION CRITERIA: Rolling correlation must remain above 0.6 for at
  least 80% of the lookback period (144 of 180 days)
- If correlation has dropped below 0.4 in the last 30 days, REJECT the pair
  (relationship may be breaking down)

Step 6: Store Qualified Pairs
- For each qualifying pair, store: hedge ratio (β), spread mean (μ), spread
  standard deviation (σ), half-life, Hurst exponent, ADF p-value, correlation
- These parameters are LOCKED until the next daily recalculation
- Maximum qualified pairs at any time: 15 (top 10 active, 5 reserve)

3.2 SPREAD MONITORING — SIGNAL GENERATION

For each qualified pair, monitor the spread in real-time on the 1-hour
timeframe:

Step 1: Calculate Current Spread
- Using the most recent 1h close prices:
  Spread = ln(Close_A) − β × ln(Close_B)
  where β is the stored hedge ratio from cointegration testing

Step 2: Calculate Z-Score
- Z = (Current_Spread − μ) / σ
  where μ is the rolling 50-period mean of the spread (on 1h candles)
  and σ is the rolling 50-period standard deviation

Step 3: Signal Detection
- LONG SPREAD signal (buy A, sell B): Z < −2.0
  (Spread has contracted significantly below its mean)
- SHORT SPREAD signal (sell A, buy B): Z > +2.0
  (Spread has expanded significantly above its mean)
- These thresholds are the DEFAULT. They may be adjusted per pair based on
  historical backtest optimization (range: 1.5 to 2.5)

3.3 ENTRY CONFIRMATION

After a Z-score signal is detected, confirm before entering:

Condition A: Z-Score Persistence
- The Z-score must remain beyond the threshold for 2 consecutive 1-hour closes
- A single spike and immediate reversion suggests noise, not a real divergence

Condition B: Volume Confirmation
- At least one of the two assets must show above-average 1h volume (> 1.2x
  20-period average) during the divergence
- If BOTH assets have below-average volume, REJECT (low conviction)

Condition C: No Regime Change in Progress
- Check if either asset has a major news event within 24 hours (from event
  calendar)
- Check if either asset has experienced > 10% move in the last 24 hours on
  its own (non-pair-related move suggesting structural break)
- If either condition is true, REJECT the signal

Condition D: Spread Trend
- The spread must be MOVING BACK toward the mean (Z-score at current bar is
  closer to 0 than 1 bar ago OR the rate of divergence has slowed)
- This is a "wait for the turn" filter — do NOT catch falling knives
- Specifically: if Z continues to increase in absolute value for 3 consecutive
  hours beyond the threshold, wait until it starts reversing

3.4 EXECUTION PROTOCOL

Step 1: Calculate Position Sizes (see Section 6)
  Asset A quantity and Asset B quantity based on hedge ratio β

Step 2: Simultaneous Entry
  For LONG SPREAD (Z < −2): Buy A (LONG) + Sell B (SHORT)
  For SHORT SPREAD (Z > +2): Sell A (SHORT) + Buy B (LONG)
  Both orders must be placed within 200ms of each other
  Order type: LIMIT at best bid/ask. If not filled within 60 seconds,
  convert to MARKET.

Step 3: Fill Reconciliation
  After both fills, verify actual hedge ratio:
  Actual_Ratio = (Quantity_A × Price_A) / (Quantity_B × Price_B)
  This should approximate the theoretical β
  If off by more than 5%, place a corrective order on the smaller leg

SECTION 4: EXIT LOGIC
================================================================================

4.1 MEAN REVERSION EXIT (PRIMARY)

- EXIT TRIGGER: Z-score reverts to within ±0.5 of zero
  (This is the primary profit-taking mechanism)
- For LONG SPREAD positions: exit when Z rises from < −2.0 to > −0.5
- For SHORT SPREAD positions: exit when Z falls from > +2.0 to < +0.5
- Do NOT wait for Z = 0 exactly (greedy exits miss the window)

4.2 OPPOSITE SIGNAL EXIT

- If a LONG SPREAD position is open and Z-score reaches +2.0, close and
  potentially reverse (if all entry conditions are met for the short spread)
- Same logic applies for SHORT SPREAD positions reaching −2.0

4.3 STOP LOSS — SPREAD CONTINUES DIVERGING

- Hard stop: Z-score reaches ±3.5 (spread diverged 3.5 standard deviations)
- This represents an extreme divergence that may indicate cointegration
  breakdown rather than temporary dislocation
- On stop loss, close BOTH legs immediately at market
- After stop loss, re-run cointegration test on this pair within 1 hour
  If pair no longer qualifies: remove from active universe
  If pair still qualifies: increase entry threshold to ±2.5 for next trade

4.4 TIME-BASED EXIT

- Maximum trade duration: 2 × half-life of the pair (in hours)
  Example: if half-life is 10 days, max duration is 20 days
- If half of the max duration passes without meaningful reversion
  (Z still beyond ±1.5), close 50% of the position
- At max duration, close remaining position regardless

4.5 COINTEGRATION BREAKDOWN EXIT

- If the daily cointegration recalculation (Section 3.1) shows the pair
  no longer qualifies (ADF p-value > 0.10 or half-life outside 3-30 range),
  close the position within 4 hours using LIMIT orders
- Do NOT wait for Z-score reversion if the statistical relationship is broken

4.6 EXIT EXECUTION

- Close both legs within 200ms of each other (same as entry)
- Use LIMIT orders first (60-second timeout), then MARKET if needed
- For partial exits, scale both legs proportionally to maintain the hedge ratio

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 POSITION SIZING & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital for this strategy: 25% of total account equity
- Maximum capital per pair: 5% of total account equity
- Maximum concurrent active pairs: 10 (but likely 5-8 at any time)
- All positions are quasi-market-neutral (long one, short another),
  so net directional exposure should be minimal

5.2 STOP LOSS ARCHITECTURE

Hard Stop: Z-score ±3.5 (see Section 4.3)
- Maximum dollar loss per pair trade: 2% of total account equity
- Calculate position size such that a move from entry Z (±2.0) to stop Z (±3.5)
  results in no more than 2% equity loss (accounting for both legs)

Dollar Stop: If combined PnL on both legs exceeds −1.5% of equity, close
regardless of Z-score

Trailing: Not applicable in traditional sense. Instead, use Z-score based:
- If Z-score reverts past zero and then starts diverging again (re-crossing
  ±0.5 away from zero), close to protect profit

5.3 DRAWDOWN PROTECTION

Daily: 2.5% halt
Weekly: 5% halt
Monthly: 8% halt
System-wide: 15% total halt (across all strategies)

5.4 EXPOSURE LIMITS

- Maximum LONG exposure (this strategy): 15% of account equity
- Maximum SHORT exposure (this strategy): 15% of account equity
- Maximum NET directional exposure (this strategy): 5% of account equity
  (since positions are paired, net should be near zero, but hedge ratio
  imperfections create residual directional exposure)
- Maximum exposure to a single asset across ALL pairs: 10% of equity
  (e.g., if BTC appears in 3 different pairs, combined BTC exposure ≤ 10%)

5.5 CORRELATION-AWARE SIZING

- Before entering a new pair trade, check directional overlap with existing
  pair trades
- If the new trade's LONG leg is in an asset already LONG in another pair:
  reduce size by 30%
- If the new trade creates net directional exposure exceeding 3% in any single
  asset: reject the trade

5.6 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

Spread Explosion:
- If Z-score reaches ±5.0 on any active pair, this indicates a structural
  break. Close ALL positions for this strategy and halt for 24 hours.

Multiple Pair Stops:
- If 3 or more pairs hit their stop losses within 24 hours, halt the entire
  strategy for 48 hours. This suggests a regime change affecting the
  cointegration relationships broadly.

Flash Crash:
- Same as STRAT-001 (10% move in 5 minutes triggers close-all)

Kill Switch:
- Closes all long and short legs across all pairs simultaneously
- Must complete within 15 seconds (more complex than single-instrument kill)

SECTION 6: POSITION SIZING RULES
================================================================================

6.1 HEDGE RATIO-BASED SIZING

The hedge ratio β from the cointegration regression determines the relative
sizes of the two legs:

Leg A (Asset A): Base_Position_USDT / Price_A = Quantity_A
Leg B (Asset B): Base_Position_USDT × β / Price_B = Quantity_B

Where:
- Base_Position_USDT = Capital_Per_Pair / 2
  (Split equally adjusted by hedge ratio for dollar-neutrality)
- Capital_Per_Pair = min(Account_Equity × 5%, strategy total ÷ active_pairs)

6.2 RISK-BASED SIZING

Alternative calculation to ensure max loss constraint:

Position_Size = Max_Loss_Allowed / Expected_Max_Spread_Move

Where:
- Max_Loss_Allowed = Account_Equity × 2% (per pair)
- Expected_Max_Spread_Move = (Stop_Z − Entry_Z) × σ_spread × β_dollar_sensitivity
  This requires converting the Z-score distance to dollar terms for the
  specific position sizes

Use the SMALLER of hedge-ratio-based and risk-based calculations.

6.3 LEVERAGE

- Maximum leverage per leg: 3x
- Preferred leverage: 2x
- Both legs combined should not exceed 3x total leverage on allocated capital

6.4 REBALANCING DURING TRADE

- If the hedge ratio shifts by more than 10% during a trade (based on
  rolling recalculation), adjust leg sizes to maintain dollar-neutrality
- Rebalancing trades must be < 20% of total position to avoid excessive costs
- Maximum rebalances per trade: 2

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 COINTEGRATION FRESHNESS
- Pairs must have been tested within the last 24 hours
- If a signal triggers on a pair whose last test is older than 24 hours,
  re-run the test before entering

7.2 SPREAD FILTER
- Both assets must have bid-ask spread < 0.05%
- If either asset's spread exceeds this, delay entry

7.3 VOLUME FILTER
- Both assets must have 24h volume > $30 million
- If either drops below, halt new entries for that pair

7.4 EXTREME MARKET CONDITIONS
- If BTC moves more than 8% in 4 hours, halt ALL pair trading for 4 hours
  (correlations distort during extreme moves, causing simultaneous losses
  across "hedged" pairs)

7.5 FEE THRESHOLD
- Expected spread reversion profit (in USDT) must exceed 3x the round-trip
  fees on BOTH legs (4 transactions total: open A, open B, close A, close B)
- Minimum expected profit per leg: entry-to-exit spread move / 2
- Total round-trip cost (4 × taker 0.04%): 0.16% of notional
- Minimum expected reversion: 0.48% of notional

7.6 CONSECUTIVE LOSS HANDLING
- After 3 consecutive losing pair trades: reduce all sizes by 25%
- After 5 consecutive losses: halt strategy, re-run all cointegration tests,
  manual review required

7.7 CORRELATION REGIME
- If the average pairwise correlation of the top 20 crypto assets exceeds 0.90
  (measured on 7-day rolling window), halt pair trading
  (In extreme correlation regimes, spreads stop mean-reverting because
  everything moves together)

SECTION 9: PAPER TRADING SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

9.1 FILL SIMULATION
- Simulate fills on BOTH legs independently using order book depth
- Model execution lag between legs (100-300ms random delay)
- Track simulated slippage per leg

9.2 FEES
- Futures taker: 0.04% per leg × 4 legs = 0.16% round-trip per pair trade
- Include in all PnL calculations

9.3 GO-LIVE CRITERIA (60-day minimum)
- Minimum 30 completed pair trades
- Net positive PnL after fees
- Profit factor > 1.3
- Win rate > 50% (mean reversion should win more often than momentum)
- Maximum drawdown < 8%
- Sharpe ratio > 1.0
- No cointegration breakdowns causing > 3% loss on a single pair

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 COINTEGRATION BREAKDOWN DURING TRADE
- The spread may not revert if the fundamental relationship has changed
  (e.g., a hard fork, regulatory action affecting one asset, major partnership)
- Detection: Z-score continues diverging beyond ±4.0 with no reversal signs
- Response: Triggered by stop at ±3.5, confirmed by daily requalification
- Post-mortem: Mark pair as "broken" for 30 days, do not re-test until then

11.2 ONE LEG FILLS, OTHER FAILS
- Same protocol as STRAT-002 Section 11.1
- Additional complexity: the unfilled leg leaves the other directionally exposed
- Maximum acceptable time with one leg open: 120 seconds (pairs are less time-
  critical than identical-asset arb)

11.3 SPREAD MEAN/VARIANCE REGIME SHIFT
- If the spread's rolling mean (μ) shifts by more than 2σ over 7 days,
  the "mean" the strategy is reverting to has moved
- Response: Recalculate μ and σ using only the most recent 50 hours of data
- If the new parameters would not have generated the current signal, exit

11.4 CORRELATION SPIKE TO 1.0 ACROSS ALL PAIRS
- During major market events, all crypto correlates to ~1.0
- All pair spreads may simultaneously diverge, causing losses across the board
- The circuit breaker in Section 5.6 (3 pair stops in 24h) handles this

11.5 INSUFFICIENT LIQUIDITY ON ONE LEG
- If one asset in a pair becomes illiquid (bid-ask > 0.2%), the pair trade
  cannot be efficiently executed or closed
- Response: Begin closing the position using LIMIT orders only, over a 4-hour
  window with gradual TWAP (Time-Weighted Average Price) execution
