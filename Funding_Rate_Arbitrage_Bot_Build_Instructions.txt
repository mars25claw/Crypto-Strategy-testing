SECTION 1: STRATEGY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 WHAT THIS STRATEGY DOES

This strategy captures the funding rate premium paid by perpetual futures
long holders to short holders. It maintains a delta-neutral position by
simultaneously holding LONG spot and SHORT perpetual futures of the same
asset in equal notional amounts. The profit comes exclusively from funding
rate payments, not from directional price movement.

1.2 MARKET CONDITIONS TARGETED

- Bullish or neutral market sentiment where perpetual futures trade at a
  premium to spot (positive funding rate)
- Periods of high leveraged long demand (strong positive funding)
- Avoids periods of negative or near-zero funding rates
- Works best during sustained uptrends where retail longs dominate

1.3 THEORETICAL EDGE

- Funding rates are structurally positive: data shows positive funding over
  92% of the time, with a baseline of +0.01% per 8-hour period embedded in
  the Binance funding formula
- Annualized baseline yield: 0.01% × 3 × 365 = 10.95%
- During bullish periods, rates frequently spike to 0.05-0.3% per 8 hours
  (annualized 54-328%)
- The edge is market-structure driven, not directional, making it one of the
  most consistent strategies available
- BIS Working Paper documents average annualized crypto carry of 6-8%
  (2019-2024), frequently exceeding 20% during demand surges

1.4 TARGET INSTRUMENTS

Primary: BTC/USDT (spot + BTCUSDT perpetual)
Secondary: ETH/USDT (spot + ETHUSDT perpetual)
Tertiary: SOL/USDT, BNB/USDT, XRP/USDT (spot + perpetual)
Maximum instruments: 5 simultaneously
Selection criteria: instruments with highest current predicted funding AND
sufficient spot + futures liquidity

1.5 FUNDING RATE MECHANICS

- Binance funding payments occur every 8 hours at 00:00, 08:00, 16:00 UTC
- Payment = Position Notional × Funding Rate
- If funding rate is POSITIVE: longs pay shorts (profitable for this strategy)
- If funding rate is NEGATIVE: shorts pay longs (costly for this strategy)
- Funding rate = Premium Index + clamp(Interest Rate - Premium Index, ±0.05%)
- Interest Rate component = 0.01% per 8 hours (structural positive bias)
- The predicted next funding rate is available in real-time via WebSocket

SECTION 3: ENTRY LOGIC
================================================================================

3.1 FUNDING RATE THRESHOLD FOR ENTRY

The strategy enters a position ONLY when the predicted funding rate exceeds
a minimum threshold that ensures profitability after all costs.

Step 1: Calculate Minimum Viable Funding Rate
- Total round-trip cost = Spot entry fee + Spot exit fee + Futures entry fee
  + Futures exit fee + estimated slippage
- Spot fees: Taker 0.10% (or current tier) × 2 legs = 0.20%
- Futures fees: Taker 0.04% (or current tier) × 2 legs = 0.08%
- Estimated slippage: 0.02% per leg × 4 legs = 0.08%
- Total round-trip cost: approximately 0.36%
- Minimum position holding periods: 3 funding periods (24 hours)
- Minimum funding rate to cover costs: 0.36% / 3 = 0.12% per period
- Add safety margin of 50%: Minimum entry threshold = 0.18% per 8-hour period
  (annualized ~197%)

Step 2: Dynamic Threshold Adjustment
- The 0.18% threshold above is the AGGRESSIVE entry threshold
- STANDARD entry threshold: predicted funding rate > 0.05% per period
  (expected holding time: 7+ funding periods to recoup costs)
- CONSERVATIVE entry threshold: predicted funding rate > 0.03% per period
  (expected holding time: 12+ funding periods to recoup costs)
- Default mode: STANDARD (0.05%)
- Mode selection is configurable via dashboard

Step 3: Funding Rate Trend Check
- Calculate the 24-hour average funding rate (last 3 funding periods)
- Calculate the 7-day average funding rate (last 21 funding periods)
- ENTRY CONDITION: Predicted rate > threshold AND 24h average > 50% of threshold
  AND 7-day average is positive
- This prevents entering on a single spike that may not sustain

3.2 BASIS SPREAD CHECK

Step 4: Calculate Current Basis
- Basis = (Futures Mark Price − Spot Index Price) / Spot Index Price × 100%
- Fetch from markPrice stream: markPrice vs indexPrice fields
- ENTRY CONDITION: Basis must be POSITIVE (futures trading at premium to spot)
  AND Basis > 0.01% (not effectively zero)

Step 5: Basis Trend
- Track basis values every minute for the last 24 hours
- Calculate 1-hour and 4-hour rolling average basis
- ENTRY CONDITION: 1-hour average basis must be positive AND not declining
  (current hour > previous hour, or stable within 0.01%)
- If basis is contracting rapidly (dropped more than 50% in 4 hours), do NOT
  enter — the premium is evaporating

3.3 SIMULTANEOUS EXECUTION PROTOCOL

The critical challenge is executing both legs (spot buy + futures short)
simultaneously to minimize basis risk during entry.

Step 6: Pre-Entry Checks
- Verify sufficient USDT balance in BOTH spot and futures wallets
- Verify both order books have adequate depth at current levels:
  Required depth at best 3 levels > 2x intended order size
- Verify bid-ask spread on both spot and futures is < 0.05% of mid-price
- Verify system clock is synchronized with Binance (offset < 500ms)

Step 7: Execution Sequence
- Calculate exact quantities for both legs:
  Spot: Buy X units of the base asset (e.g., 0.1 BTC)
  Futures: Short X units equivalent notional in perpetual contract
  Quantities must match to within 0.1% of notional value
- Place BOTH orders within a 100ms window:
  Order 1: Spot MARKET buy
  Order 2: Futures MARKET sell (open short)
- If either order fails to submit within 100ms, immediately cancel the other
  if not yet filled

Step 8: Fill Reconciliation
- After both fills confirmed, calculate actual basis captured:
  Actual Basis = (Futures Fill Price − Spot Fill Price) / Spot Fill Price
- Log: intended basis, actual basis, slippage per leg, total execution cost
- If actual basis is more than 0.05% worse than intended, flag for review
  but do NOT unwind (the position is still likely profitable)

Step 9: Delta Neutrality Verification
- After entry, verify the position is delta-neutral:
  Spot notional = Spot quantity × Spot price
  Futures notional = Futures quantity × Futures mark price
  Delta = Spot notional − abs(Futures notional)
- Acceptable delta: within 0.5% of total notional
- If delta exceeds 0.5%, place a corrective order on the leg that is smaller

SECTION 4: EXIT LOGIC
================================================================================

4.1 FUNDING RATE REVERSAL EXIT

- Monitor predicted funding rate continuously via markPrice stream
- EXIT TRIGGER: Predicted funding rate turns NEGATIVE (below −0.005%)
  AND stays negative for 2 consecutive funding periods
- For STANDARD mode: Exit if predicted rate drops below +0.01% for 3
  consecutive periods (below the structural baseline — not worth the capital)
- For AGGRESSIVE mode: Exit only if rate goes negative
- For CONSERVATIVE mode: Exit if predicted rate drops below +0.02% for 2
  consecutive periods

4.2 BASIS CONVERGENCE/INVERSION EXIT

- EXIT TRIGGER: Basis (futures − spot) drops below −0.05% (futures trading
  at discount to spot — inversion)
- If basis inverts, the strategy is paying for the inversion AND potentially
  facing negative funding — exit immediately
- Gradual exit: If basis drops below +0.01% for 4+ hours, begin unwinding
  25% of position every 2 hours

4.3 SIMULTANEOUS UNWINDING PROTOCOL

Exit must close both legs simultaneously to maintain delta neutrality:

Step 1: Place both closing orders within a 100ms window:
  Spot: MARKET sell all held units
  Futures: MARKET buy to close short position
Step 2: If one leg fills but the other fails:
  - Retry the unfilled leg up to 5 times with 200ms intervals
  - If still unfilled, this is a CRITICAL situation: the position is now
    directionally exposed. Place the order as an aggressive LIMIT order
    at best bid/ask and wait up to 30 seconds.
  - If still unfilled after 30 seconds, execute as MARKET with no price limit
  - Alert operator immediately

4.4 PROFIT TARGET EXIT

- This strategy does NOT use fixed profit targets (it aims to hold as long as
  funding is favorable)
- However, if cumulative funding earned on the position exceeds 5% of position
  notional AND the current funding rate has dropped below the entry threshold,
  consider this a complete trade and exit

4.5 TIME-BASED REVIEW

- Every 24 hours, evaluate whether the position should remain open:
  - Is the 24h average funding rate still above the minimum threshold?
  - Is the basis still positive?
  - Is the annualized yield (funding earned / notional / holding_days × 365)
    still attractive (> 8% annualized)?
- If the answer to any question is NO for 3 consecutive daily reviews, exit

4.6 MAXIMUM HOLDING PERIOD

- Maximum holding period: 30 days per position
- After 30 days, close the position regardless of funding conditions
- Wait 24 hours, then re-evaluate for fresh entry
- This prevents excessive counterparty exposure to a single exchange and
  forces periodic reconciliation

4.7 PRE-FUNDING SETTLEMENT EXIT (TACTICAL)

- When funding rate for the upcoming period is predicted to be extremely
  negative (below −0.1%), consider temporarily closing the futures short
  BEFORE the funding timestamp and reopening AFTER
- This micro-optimization must account for the round-trip cost of closing
  and reopening the futures leg (2 × taker fee = 0.08%)
- Only execute if: abs(predicted negative funding) × notional > round-trip cost

SECTION 5: RISK MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 POSITION SIZING & CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- Maximum capital allocated to this strategy: 40% of total account equity
  (higher than directional strategies because this is delta-neutral)
- Maximum per-instrument allocation: 20% of total account equity
- Capital split between wallets:
  Spot wallet: 55% of allocated capital (to buy spot assets)
  Futures wallet: 45% of allocated capital (margin for short positions)
  The split accounts for the fact that futures positions require less margin
  (leveraged) but need buffer for mark-to-market movements

5.2 LEVERAGE ON FUTURES LEG

- Maximum leverage on the futures short: 3x
- Preferred leverage: 2x
- Rationale: Even though the position is delta-neutral, the futures leg can
  be liquidated if price rallies strongly AND the spot profit cannot offset
  futures margin calls in real-time (because they are in different wallets)
- Lower leverage provides larger buffer against liquidation of the short leg

5.3 LIQUIDATION DISTANCE MONITORING

- Calculate liquidation price of the futures short position continuously:
  Liq Price = Entry Price × (1 + (Margin / Notional) × (1 − Maintenance Margin Rate))
  (Simplified — use Binance's exact formula with tiered margin rates)
- Monitor the distance between current price and liquidation price
- WARNING threshold: price is within 15% of liquidation price
  Action: Transfer additional USDT from spot to futures wallet
- CRITICAL threshold: price is within 10% of liquidation price
  Action: Partially close BOTH legs (25% of position) to free margin
- EMERGENCY threshold: price is within 5% of liquidation price
  Action: Close ENTIRE position immediately (both legs)

5.4 CROSS-WALLET MARGIN MANAGEMENT

- Maintain a reserve buffer of 10% of total allocated capital in the futures
  wallet beyond what is needed for margin
- When the futures wallet balance (excluding unrealized PnL) drops below 20%
  of the total short position notional, trigger a margin top-up:
  Sell a portion of spot holdings → transfer USDT to futures wallet
- This creates a temporary delta imbalance that must be corrected within 1 hour
  by reducing the futures short proportionally

5.5 DRAWDOWN PROTECTION

Daily Drawdown Limit: 1.5% of starting-of-day equity
- This strategy should have very low drawdowns given its delta-neutral nature
- If daily drawdown exceeds 1.5%, it indicates a structural problem (basis
  collapse, liquidation risk, execution failure). HALT immediately.

Weekly Drawdown Limit: 3%
Monthly Drawdown Limit: 5%
System-Wide: Same as Section 5 of STRAT-001 (15% total halt)

5.6 EXPOSURE LIMITS

- Maximum concurrent instruments for this strategy: 5
- Maximum single-asset notional: 20% of total account equity
- Maximum total strategy notional (sum of all instruments): 80% of allocated
  capital (40% of total equity × 2 because each position has two legs)
- Delta must remain within ±0.5% at all times

5.7 CIRCUIT BREAKERS

Basis Flash Inversion:
- If basis inverts (negative) by more than 0.5% within 5 minutes,
  close ALL positions for this strategy immediately (both legs)
- Cool-down: 4 hours before re-entry

Funding Rate Shock:
- If predicted funding rate swings from positive to below −0.2% within a
  single period, close all positions
- Cool-down: 24 hours

Liquidation Cascade Detection:
- If forceOrder stream shows more than 50 liquidation events in 60 seconds
  for a held instrument, prepare for emergency exit
- If liquidation volume exceeds 10% of 24h trading volume in 5 minutes,
  execute emergency exit of ALL positions

Exchange Anomaly:
- If spot price and futures mark price diverge by more than 2% for more than
  60 seconds (outside of known volatility events), halt and alert

Kill Switch:
- Same as STRAT-001: manual and automated, completes within 10 seconds
- Kill switch must close BOTH spot and futures legs

SECTION 6: POSITION SIZING RULES
================================================================================

6.1 BASE CALCULATION

Spot_Position_Size = Allocated_Capital × Spot_Split / Spot_Price
Futures_Position_Size = Spot_Position_Size (matched notional)
Futures_Margin_Required = Futures_Position_Size × Futures_Price / Leverage

Where:
- Allocated_Capital = min(Account_Equity × 40%, per-instrument cap)
- Spot_Split = 55%
- Futures_Split = 45%

Verify: Futures_Margin_Required < Futures_Wallet_Balance × 80% (20% buffer)

6.2 INSTRUMENT SELECTION AND ALLOCATION

When multiple instruments have qualifying funding rates:

Step 1: Rank all qualifying instruments by predicted annualized yield:
  Yield = Predicted_Funding_Rate × 3 × 365 × 100%

Step 2: Allocate capital proportionally to yield, with caps:
  Instrument 1 (highest yield): up to 20% of total equity
  Instrument 2: up to 15%
  Instrument 3: up to 10%
  Instruments 4-5: up to 5% each

Step 3: Round position sizes to exchange minimum lot sizes
Step 4: Verify total allocation does not exceed strategy maximum (40%)

6.3 REBALANCING

- Rebalance allocations weekly (every Monday 00:00 UTC)
- If a held instrument's funding rate drops below threshold but another
  qualifying instrument has higher yield, rotate capital
- Rotation must be delta-neutral at all times: close both legs of outgoing
  instrument before opening both legs of incoming instrument
- Maximum rotation per week: 2 instruments (to control transaction costs)

SECTION 7: FILTERS & DISQUALIFIERS
================================================================================

7.1 MINIMUM LIQUIDITY
- Spot 24h volume must exceed $50 million
- Futures 24h volume must exceed $200 million
- If either drops below these thresholds while in a position, begin unwinding
  over 24 hours (close 25% every 6 hours)

7.2 SPREAD FILTER
- Spot bid-ask spread must be < 0.05% of mid-price
- Futures bid-ask spread must be < 0.03% of mid-price
- If spread exceeds these during entry window, delay entry until compliant

7.3 FUNDING RATE SUSTAINABILITY
- Do NOT enter if the high funding rate is caused by a single 8-hour spike
  (current rate > 3x the 7-day average with no sustained elevation)
- Require at least 3 of the last 6 funding periods to be above the entry
  threshold before committing capital

7.4 MARKET REGIME FILTER
- During extreme fear events (BTC down > 15% in 7 days), funding rates may
  be artificially depressed and volatile. Pause new entries.
- During extreme greed (BTC up > 30% in 7 days), funding rates are likely
  elevated but the risk of a sharp reversal (basis collapse) increases.
  Enter with 50% reduced size.

7.5 FEE THRESHOLD
- Minimum expected funding income over planned holding period must exceed
  3x the total round-trip transaction costs (both legs)
- If estimated holding period at current rate produces less than 3x fees,
  the trade is not viable — skip it

7.6 EXCHANGE COUNTERPARTY DIVERSIFICATION
- No more than 60% of total funding arb capital should be on a single exchange
- If implementing on multiple exchanges, ensure withdrawal paths are clear
  and tested (this is an architectural note for future multi-exchange versions)

7.7 CONSECUTIVE LOSS HANDLING
- After 2 consecutive losing positions (net negative after funding and fees):
  Reduce position size by 50% for next entry
- After 4 consecutive losses: HALT strategy, manual review required
  (4 losses in a delta-neutral strategy indicates a systemic issue)

SECTION 8: 24/7 OPERATION HANDLING
================================================================================

8.1 STATE PERSISTENCE

Persist every 5 seconds:
- All open positions: BOTH spot and futures legs with exact quantities, entry
  prices, entry timestamps, accumulated funding income per position
- Wallet balances: spot USDT, spot asset quantities, futures wallet balance,
  futures position margin, futures unrealized PnL
- Funding tracking: total funding earned per instrument, per day, per period
- Predicted next funding rate and timestamp
- Basis history: last 24 hours of basis values (1-minute resolution)
- Delta calculation: current net delta exposure per instrument

8.2 STARTUP RECONCILIATION

Same protocol as STRAT-001 Section 8.2, with additional steps:
- Reconcile BOTH spot and futures account states
- Verify delta neutrality: for each instrument, spot holdings × spot price
  must approximately equal futures short notional
- Check if any funding payments were received during downtime:
  Query GET /fapi/v1/income with incomeType=FUNDING_FEE for the downtime period
- Update cumulative funding income tracking
- If a spot holding exists without a matching futures short (or vice versa),
  this is a CRITICAL orphan situation:
  - If spot exists without futures short: immediately open matching futures short
  - If futures short exists without spot: immediately buy matching spot
  - Both corrections must happen within 60 seconds of detection

8.3 FUNDING SETTLEMENT MONITORING

- At each funding timestamp (00:00, 08:00, 16:00 UTC), the bot must:
  1. Record the actual funding rate applied
  2. Query /fapi/v1/income for the funding payment received/paid
  3. Compare actual payment with expected payment (position × rate)
  4. Log any discrepancy exceeding 0.1% of expected
  5. Update cumulative performance tracking
- Perform this check within 5 minutes of each funding timestamp
- Set a timer for 10 minutes before each funding timestamp to verify
  positions are correctly held (no accidental closures)

8.4 WALLET REBALANCING AUTOMATION

- Monitor spot and futures wallet balance ratios every 5 minutes
- If futures wallet balance drops below required margin × 1.3 (30% buffer
  eroding), initiate automated transfer:
  - Sell a calculated amount of spot asset
  - Transfer USDT to futures wallet via internal transfer
  - Reduce futures short proportionally to maintain delta neutrality
  - Log the rebalancing event with all details

8.5 HEARTBEAT, MEMORY, LOG ROTATION

Same specifications as STRAT-001 Section 8.3-8.5, with strategy ID STRAT-002.

SECTION 9: PAPER TRADING SPECIFICATIONS
================================================================================

9.1 FUNDING PAYMENT SIMULATION

- At each funding timestamp, calculate and credit the simulated funding payment:
  Payment = Futures_Position_Notional × Actual_Funding_Rate
- If funding rate is positive, credit the paper account (short earns)
- If funding rate is negative, debit the paper account (short pays)
- Use the ACTUAL funding rate from Binance, not predicted (simulate reality)

9.2 EXECUTION SIMULATION

- Simulate both spot and futures fills independently
- Spot fill: best ask + simulated slippage (walk order book depth)
- Futures fill: best bid + simulated slippage
- Simulate 200ms delay between legs (realistic execution gap)
- Track basis captured vs intended for each entry/exit

9.3 FEE STRUCTURE

- Spot: Taker 0.10% per trade (or applicable tier)
- Futures: Taker 0.04% per trade (or applicable tier)
- Apply to all four legs (spot open, spot close, futures open, futures close)

9.4 GO-LIVE CRITERIA

Minimum 30-day paper trading period, with:
- Net positive PnL after all simulated fees and funding
- Annualized yield > 8% (after costs)
- Maximum drawdown < 3%
- No instances of delta exceeding ±1% for more than 5 minutes
- Successful handling of at least 2 negative funding rate periods
- Successful simulation of at least 1 entry and 1 exit
- Uptime > 98%

SECTION 11: EDGE CASES & FAILURE MODES
================================================================================

11.1 ONE LEG FILLS, OTHER FAILS
- CRITICAL scenario. If spot buy fills but futures short fails (or vice versa):
  Immediate retry of failed leg: 5 attempts, 200ms apart
  If still failing: attempt to close the filled leg at market
  If BOTH legs are stuck (one filled, one failing, and the filled one cannot
  close): this is an EMERGENCY. Alert operator immediately. The position is
  now directionally exposed.
  Maximum time to remain with one leg open: 60 seconds
  Fallback: accept the directional exposure and place a tight stop loss
  (1% from current price) on the open leg until the situation is resolved

11.2 FUNDING RATE TURNS SHARPLY NEGATIVE DURING HOLDING
- If predicted funding rate drops below −0.05%:
  Evaluate cost of paying one negative funding period vs cost of closing
  and reopening later
  If negative_rate × notional < round_trip_cost: absorb the payment
  If negative_rate × notional > round_trip_cost: close futures short before
  settlement, reopen after settlement (tactical avoidance)
  If rate stays negative for 3+ periods: full exit per Section 4.1

11.3 SHARP PRICE RALLY THREATENING FUTURES LIQUIDATION
- The spot position GAINS value during rallies, but those gains are in the
  spot wallet and cannot directly offset futures margin calls
- The bot must proactively transfer USDT to futures wallet during rallies:
  Monitor: If price increases by 5%+ from short entry, initiate transfer
  Sell spot proportionally, transfer to futures, or use available USDT
- If Binance internal transfers are delayed or failing: reduce futures
  position size immediately to lower margin requirements

11.4 SPOT ASSET DELISTED OR HALTED
- If a spot asset is halted for trading on Binance:
  Close the futures leg immediately (it will be directionally exposed anyway)
  Alert operator with full position details
  Track the halted asset in state for eventual resolution

11.5 BINANCE FEE TIER CHANGE
- Query current fee tier on startup and every 24 hours
- If fees increase, recalculate minimum viable funding rate threshold
- Adjust entry threshold dynamically to maintain profitability

11.6 CLOCK DRIFT CAUSING MISSED FUNDING SETTLEMENT
- If the bot's clock drifts and it closes/reopens around a funding timestamp
  incorrectly, it may miss a funding payment or pay an unwanted one
- Synchronize with Binance server time every 60 seconds
- Never execute position changes within 5 minutes of a funding timestamp
  (00:00, 08:00, 16:00 UTC) unless it is a tactical funding avoidance trade
